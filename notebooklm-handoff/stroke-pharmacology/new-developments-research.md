## TOPIC 1: DOACs vs Warfarin -- Long-Term and Real-World Data

### What the App Currently Covers
The app has comprehensive coverage of the four landmark DOAC trials (RE-LY, ROCKET-AF, ARISTOTLE, ENGAGE AF) with a comparison table.

### New Developments (2020-2026)

**Patient-Level Network Meta-Analysis (Circulation, 2022)** [BOARD-CORE]
- Combined data from 71,683 participants across all four DOAC trials
- DOACs reduce stroke/systemic embolism by 19% vs warfarin (RR 0.81, 95% CI 0.73-0.91, p<0.0001)
- Consistent hemorrhagic stroke reduction across all DOACs vs warfarin
- No significant interaction by age or sex
- Source: Circulation 2022; doi:10.1161/CIRCULATIONAHA.121.056355

**Meta-Analysis in Low-Risk AF** [BOARD-CORE]
- In AF patients with low stroke risk, DOACs still associated with significantly lower stroke, major bleeding, ICH, and mortality vs warfarin
- Supports DOAC use even in lower CHA2DS2-VASc scores
- Source: PubMed 2023; PMID 37573616

**Meta-Analysis in Elderly Patients (2025)** [BOARD-LIKELY]
- Systematic review confirms DOAC benefit maintained in elderly AF patients
- Source: PMC 2025; PMC12820896

**Gap Identified:** App should note the meta-analysis level evidence confirming DOACs' class-wide superiority for hemorrhagic stroke reduction, not just trial-by-trial comparisons.

## TOPIC 2: Dual Antiplatelet Therapy Duration and New Agents

### What the App Currently Covers
Excellent coverage of CHANCE and POINT trials with 21-day DAPT recommendation. COMPASS and NAVIGATE ESUS also covered.

### New Developments (2020-2026)

**THALES Trial (NEJM 2020)** [BOARD-LIKELY]
- 11,016 patients with minor stroke (NIHSS <=5) or high-risk TIA (ABCD2 >=6)
- Aspirin + ticagrelor for 30 days vs aspirin alone
- Reduced stroke and death but increased severe bleeding
- Benefit concentrated in first week
- Provides ticagrelor-based alternative to clopidogrel-based DAPT
- Source: NEJM 2020; THALES Trial

**CHANCE-2 Trial (NEJM 2021)** [BOARD-LIKELY]
- First pharmacogenomic-guided antiplatelet trial in stroke
- CYP2C19 loss-of-function carriers with minor stroke/TIA
- Ticagrelor-aspirin superior to clopidogrel-aspirin for 90-day stroke risk
- No increase in severe/moderate bleeding
- Supports precision medicine approach: test CYP2C19, switch to ticagrelor if LOF carrier
- Source: NEJM 2021; doi:10.1056/NEJMoa2111749

**European Stroke Organisation Guidelines (2021)** [BOARD-CORE]
- Strong recommendation: 21-day ASA + clopidogrel for minor stroke/high-risk TIA within 24h
- Weak recommendation: 30-day ASA + ticagrelor as alternative
- Source: PMC 2021; PMC8370083

**Meta-Analysis of Timing, Duration, and Regimen (Frontiers 2025)** [BOARD-LIKELY]
- Comprehensive meta-analysis confirming optimal DAPT parameters
- Source: Frontiers in Pharmacology 2025

**NEJM 2024: DAPT up to 72 Hours After Stroke** [BOARD-LIKELY]
- Extended initiation window for DAPT to 72 hours post-stroke showing benefit
- Source: NEJM 2024; doi:10.1056/NEJMoa2309137

**Gap Identified:** App needs THALES and CHANCE-2 trial cards. The pharmacogenomic angle (CYP2C19 testing) is increasingly board-relevant as precision medicine gains traction.

## TOPIC 3: Statin Therapy -- LDL Targets

### What the App Currently Covers
Good coverage of SPARCL trial, statin pleiotropic effects, intensity classification, and the "do not stop statins" pearl.

### New Developments (2020-2026)

**Treat Stroke to Target (TST) Trial (NEJM 2020)** [BOARD-CORE]
- 2,860 patients with atherosclerotic stroke/TIA
- LDL target <70 mg/dL vs 90-110 mg/dL
- Mean achieved: 65 vs 96 mg/dL
- Composite primary endpoint: 8.5% vs 10.9% (HR 0.78, P=0.04) -- 22% relative risk reduction
- No increase in ICH or new-onset diabetes
- Statin + ezetimibe combination used when needed to achieve targets
- THIS IS THE DEFINITIVE TRIAL establishing LDL <70 as the target for atherosclerotic stroke
- Source: NEJM 2020; doi:10.1056/NEJMoa1910355

**SPARCL Post-Hoc Analysis (JACC 2020)** [BOARD-CORE]
- Atorvastatin reduced first AND subsequent vascular events across vascular territories
- Source: JACC 2020; doi:10.1016/j.jacc.2020.03.015

**JAMA Neurology Meta-Analysis (2022)** [BOARD-CORE]
- Meta-analysis of RCTs: intensity of LDL reduction directly associated with secondary stroke prevention benefit
- Dose-response relationship confirmed
- Source: JAMA Neurology 2022

**Gap Identified:** TST trial is a major addition the app currently lacks. This trial directly answers the question "what LDL should I target?" -- highly board-relevant. Ezetimibe as add-on should also be mentioned.

## TOPIC 4: Blood Pressure Targets Post-Stroke

### What the App Currently Covers
Coverage of PROGRESS trial, SPS3 trial, general BP targets. Mentions <130/80 target.

### New Developments (2020-2026)

**2021 AHA/ASA Secondary Prevention Guidelines** [BOARD-CORE]
- Formal recommendation: target BP <130/80 mmHg for secondary stroke prevention
- Based on meta-analysis of 4 RCTs (RESPECT, PAST-BP, SPS3, PODCAST)
- Significant 22% reduction in recurrent stroke with intensive control (RR 0.78)
- Source: Stroke 2021; doi:10.1161/STR.0000000000000375

**J-Shaped Relationship Evidence** [BOARD-CORE]
- Optimal BP approximately SBP 124, DBP 67 mmHg
- Risk increases both above and below these values
- SBP <120 mmHg should be avoided -- diminishing returns with increased hypotension risk
- Source: Hypertension 2016; AHA Journals

**ESPRIT Trial Secondary Analysis (2025)** [BOARD-EMERGING]
- Intensive SBP (mean 119 mmHg) vs standard (mean 135 mmHg)
- Stroke: 4.7% intensive vs 5.4% standard
- Benefit mainly from hemorrhagic stroke reduction
- Supports intensive targets but reinforces floor effect
- Source: JACC 2025

**2024-2025 International Guidelines Comparison** [BOARD-EMERGING]
- 2023 ESH: <130/80, avoid <120
- 2024 ESC: SBP 120-129
- Convergence toward lower targets with recognized floor
- Source: Hypertension Research 2025

**Gap Identified:** App should add the 2021 AHA/ASA formal guideline recommendation and the J-shaped relationship evidence with optimal SBP approximately 124 mmHg.

## TOPIC 5: Anticoagulant Reversal Agents -- Updated Evidence

### What the App Currently Covers
Excellent reversal agent table with dosing. Specificity pearl about idarucizumab and andexanet alfa.

### New Developments (2020-2026)

**ANNEXA-4 Trial Updated Results** [BOARD-LIKELY]
- Good/excellent hemostasis in 83% within 12 hours
- Thrombotic events: 11% at 30 days -- significantly higher than idarucizumab or PCC
- Cost: approximately $50,000 per high-dose treatment
- Many hospitals do not stock andexanet alfa
- Source: tctmd.com; JACC 2021

**Comparative Meta-Analysis (JAMA Network Open 2022)** [BOARD-LIKELY]
- Hemostatic efficacy comparable: PCC 80%, idarucizumab 77%, andexanet 81%
- Thrombotic risk: andexanet (VTE 5.5%, ATE 5.0%) > PCC (2.4%, 2.2%) > idarucizumab (1.7%, 3.3%)
- PCC increasingly recognized as reasonable first-line alternative when specific agents unavailable
- Source: JAMA Network Open 2022

**ISTH 2024 Updated Guidance** [BOARD-LIKELY]
- New reversal recommendations published
- Emphasis on PCC as practical alternative
- Source: Journal of Thrombosis and Haemostasis 2024

**Comprehensive 2025 Review** [BOARD-LIKELY]
- Both REVERSE-AD (idarucizumab) and ANNEXA-4 lacked control groups
- Real-world evidence accumulating
- Source: British Journal of Clinical Pharmacology 2025

**Gap Identified:** App should note the thrombotic risk profile differences and cost considerations. Board questions may ask about choosing between PCC and andexanet alfa.

## TOPIC 6: SSRIs for Motor Recovery -- The Definitive Answer

### What the App Currently Covers
The app does NOT currently cover SSRIs for motor recovery -- this is a significant gap. The FLAME trial is mentioned in the Cuccurullo textbook but not in the app.

### New Developments (2020-2026)

**FOCUS Trial (Lancet 2019)** [BOARD-CORE]
- Large pragmatic RCT; fluoxetine 20 mg daily for 6 months post-stroke
- NO benefit on functional outcome (mRS) at 6 months
- Reduced depression occurrence but increased bone fracture risk
- Source: Lancet 2019; doi:10.1016/S0140-6736(18)32823-X

**AFFINITY Trial (Lancet Neurology 2020)** [BOARD-CORE]
- Fluoxetine 20 mg daily for 6 months in Australia/Vietnam/NZ
- NO improvement in functional outcome
- INCREASED falls, fractures, seizures
- Validates FOCUS in ethnically diverse population
- Source: Lancet Neurology 2020; doi:10.1016/S1474-4422(20)30207-6

**EFFECTS Trial (Lancet Neurology 2020)** [BOARD-CORE]
- Fluoxetine 20 mg daily for 6 months in Sweden
- NO effect on functional outcome up to 1 year
- Fewer depression diagnoses but increased fractures
- Source: Lancet Neurology 2020; doi:10.1016/S1474-4422(20)30219-2

**Individual Patient Data Meta-Analysis (FOCUS+AFFINITY+EFFECTS, n=3,356)** [BOARD-CORE]
- Definitive answer: NO effect of SSRIs on functional independence or disability
- SSRIs should NOT be prescribed routinely for motor recovery
- They are ineffective AND increase serious adverse events (fractures, seizures, falls)
- The original FLAME trial (n=118) was likely a false positive due to small sample size
- Source: Trials 2020 (protocol); Lancet Neurology 2020 (results)

**Board Pearl:** The narrative arc here is critical for board exams -- FLAME suggested benefit, but three large well-designed trials definitively showed NO benefit. This is a common board exam pattern: small positive trial overturned by large definitive trials.

**Gap Identified:** MAJOR gap. App needs a dedicated section on SSRIs for motor recovery covering the FLAME-to-FOCUS/AFFINITY/EFFECTS trajectory. This is highly board-testable.

## TOPIC 7: Neuroprotective Agents

### What the App Currently Covers
Not covered in the app. Cuccurullo mentions no effective agent has been established.

### New Developments (2020-2026)

**Nerinetide (ESCAPE-NA1, NEJM 2020)** [BOARD-EMERGING]
- PSD-95 inhibitor preventing excitotoxic signaling
- In patients NOT receiving alteplase: improved outcomes (59.3% vs 49.8% mRS 0-2)
- No benefit in alteplase-treated patients (alteplase cleaves nerinetide)
- Source: NEJM 2020

**2025 Meta-Analysis (3 RCTs, n=2,462)** [BOARD-EMERGING]
- Modest mortality reduction (RR 0.86, P=0.05)
- Some benefit in infarct size and stroke worsening
- Benefit may be limited to patients within 3h of onset selected for thrombectomy
- Source: PMC 2025; PMC12768193

**ACT-42 Trial (Ongoing)** [BOARD-EMERGING]
- Next-generation PSD-95 inhibitor trial in progress (NCT06403267)
- Source: ClinicalTrials.gov

**Clinical Relevance:** Nerinetide is the most promising neuroprotective agent in decades but remains investigational. Board relevance is emerging -- know that no neuroprotective agent is currently approved for clinical use.

## TOPIC 8: Anti-Spasticity Medications

### What the App Currently Covers
Not in the pharmacology module. Covered in Cuccurullo rehab sections.

### New Developments (2020-2026)

**Updated Guideline Recommendations (2024)** [BOARD-CORE]
- BoNT-A remains first-line focal treatment for post-stroke spasticity
- GRADE A treatments: stretching, BoNT-A injection, intrathecal baclofen, ESWT, peripheral magnetic stimulation
- Oral agents (baclofen, tizanidine, dantrolene) have limited evidence and significant side effects in stroke
- Source: PMC 2024; PMC10892074

**French Nationwide Study (2023)** [BOARD-LIKELY]
- Large retrospective analysis of BoNT-A use in post-stroke spasticity
- Confirms real-world patterns: upper limb most common target, repeated injections required
- Source: Frontiers in Neurology 2023

**Baclotox Trial (2025, Ongoing)** [BOARD-EMERGING]
- First head-to-head RCT of BoNT-A vs baclofen early post-stroke
- Tests whether BoNT-A better supports motor recovery than baclofen
- Source: JMIR Research Protocols 2025

**Gap Identified:** The pharmacology module should include at least a brief anti-spasticity section given its PM&R board relevance. Botulinum toxin vs oral agents is a core PM&R topic.

## TOPIC 9: Post-Stroke Depression Pharmacotherapy

### What the App Currently Covers
Not in the pharmacology module. DeLisa covers PSD treatment briefly.

### New Developments (2020-2026)

**AHA/ASA Scientific Statement on PSD** [BOARD-CORE]
- Affects approximately 33% of stroke survivors at any time point
- SSRIs are first-line; SNRIs for prominent anxiety
- Early treatment enhances physical/cognitive recovery and may improve 10-year survival
- Source: Stroke 2017; doi:10.1161/str.0000000000000113

**Prevention Trials Meta-Analysis** [BOARD-LIKELY]
- Antidepressants significantly decrease PSD incidence vs placebo
- 12-month regimens most effective (mirtazapine, milnacipran, sertraline)
- 6-month fluoxetine also effective for prevention
- Source: NDT 2023

**Safety Consideration (2024)** [BOARD-LIKELY]
- SSRIs with concurrent DAPT may increase hemorrhagic stroke risk by 29%
- Caution warranted in the 21-day DAPT window
- Source: Clinical data 2024

**Gap Identified:** PSD pharmacotherapy should be added to the pharmacology module as a board-relevant topic.

## TOPIC 10: Medications to Avoid After Stroke

### What the App Currently Covers
Not explicitly covered.

### Key Evidence [BOARD-CORE]

**Drugs that may impair neuroplasticity and recovery:**
- Clonidine, prazosin (central alpha agonists)
- Haloperidol and other typical antipsychotics (dopamine antagonists)
- Benzodiazepines (diazepam, lorazepam)
- Phenytoin, phenobarbital (older anticonvulsants)
- Source: PMC 2020; PMC7327936; Goldstein 1995 landmark paper

**Preferred alternatives:**
- Levetiracetam over phenytoin for seizure management
- Quetiapine or olanzapine over haloperidol if antipsychotic needed
- Trazodone over benzodiazepines for insomnia
- Methylphenidate or modafinil for arousal/fatigue

**Gap Identified:** The app should include a brief section on medications to avoid, as this is a classic PM&R board question.

## TOPIC 11: ELAN Trial -- Timing of Anticoagulation

### What the App Currently Covers
The 1-3-6-12 day rule is well covered.

### New Developments (2020-2026)

**ELAN Trial (NEJM 2023)** [BOARD-LIKELY]
- 103 sites in 15 countries
- Early DOAC initiation (within 48h for minor/moderate, day 6-7 for major) vs later initiation
- Similar safety and efficacy -- early initiation non-inferior
- Supports earlier DOAC start than traditional timing heuristics
- Source: NEJM 2023; doi:10.1056/NEJMoa2303048

**OPTIMAS Trial (Lancet 2024)** [BOARD-LIKELY]
- Complementary UK trial confirming ELAN findings
- Early anticoagulation safe and effective
- Source: Lancet 2024

**Clinical Impact:** Together, ELAN and OPTIMAS are shifting practice toward earlier DOAC initiation. The 1-3-6-12 rule is still taught but is being refined by these trials.
