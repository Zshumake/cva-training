## TOPIC 1: CIRCLE OF WILLIS VARIANTS AND CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE

### What the App Currently Covers
- Components of the Circle of Willis
- Only 20-25% have a complete circle
- Common variants with prevalence and clinical implications
- PComm hypoplasia is most common variant

### New Findings (2020-2026)

**1. Incomplete Circle and Stroke Outcomes** [BOARD-LIKELY]
A 2022 study (European Radiology, PMID 35661459) found that incomplete Circle of Willis variants are associated with significantly lower odds of good short-term stroke outcomes, even after adjusting for age and NIHSS. This is clinically relevant because it suggests collateral anatomy affects prognosis independent of stroke severity.

**Verification**: Confirmed by a 2023 PMC systematic review (PMC 9948548) and a 2024 Ethiopian hospital cohort study (PMC 11846220).

**2. Circle of Willis in M1 Occlusion** [BOARD-LIKELY]
A 2021 study (PMC 8596978) specifically examined CoW variants in MCA-M1 occlusion and found that CoW configuration does NOT play a significant role in M1 occlusion outcomes. This suggests leptomeningeal collaterals are more important than CoW anatomy for distal occlusions.

**3. Prevalence Data Update** [BOARD-CORE]
A 2024 systematic review (JMCHEM Sci) analyzing 525 healthy patients via 3D TOF MRA confirmed only 20.9% had a complete Circle of Willis, validating the textbook figure.

### GAP IDENTIFIED
The app does not discuss the prognostic implications of CoW completeness for stroke outcomes. Consider adding: "Patients with an incomplete Circle of Willis have worse short-term stroke outcomes, but this effect is more important for proximal (ICA/carotid T) occlusions than for distal (M1) occlusions."

## TOPIC 2: ANTERIOR VS POSTERIOR CIRCULATION -- UPDATED DISTINCTIONS

### What the App Currently Covers
- Comprehensive territory mapping for MCA, ACA, PCA
- Brainstem syndrome presentations
- "Crossed signs" concept for posterior circulation

### New Findings (2020-2026)

**1. Posterior Circulation Stroke Diagnosis and Management** [BOARD-LIKELY]
A 2024 comprehensive review (PMC 11046504) confirmed that posterior circulation stroke diagnosis remains challenging. Highly specific signs for posterior circulation include Horner syndrome, crossed sensory deficits, quadrantanopia, and oculomotor nerve palsy -- but all have very low sensitivity (1.3-4.0%).

**2. Inflammatory Signatures Differ** [BOARD-EMERGING]
A 2025 Frontiers in Medicine study found distinct clinical and inflammatory signatures between anterior and posterior circulation strokes, suggesting underlying biological differences that could eventually enable biomarker-based classification.

**3. Post-Stroke Dizziness in Anterior Circulation** [BOARD-EMERGING]
A 2026 Frontiers in Neurology study documented that dizziness can occur in anterior circulation strokes (not just posterior), because the human vestibular cortical network involves parietal cortex and insula, which are in MCA territory.

**Verification**: Consistent with established neuroscience regarding distributed vestibular processing.

### GAP IDENTIFIED
The app could add a pearl: "Dizziness is not exclusively a posterior circulation sign -- anterior circulation strokes involving the parietal cortex or insula can also cause vestibular symptoms."

## TOPIC 3: WATERSHED INFARCTS -- MECHANISMS AND IMAGING

### What the App Currently Covers
- Three watershed zones with presentations
- "Man in a barrel" syndrome
- Causes (systemic hypotension, bilateral carotid stenosis)
- Transcortical aphasias

### New Findings (2020-2026)

**1. Dual Mechanism Clarification** [BOARD-CORE]
A 2023 PMC narrative review (PMC 10478671) and a 2023 Annals of Indian Academy of Neurology review confirmed: External (cortical) watershed infarcts are primarily embolic; internal (subcortical) watershed infarcts are primarily hemodynamic. This distinction has therapeutic implications.

**2. "String of Pearls" Sign** [BOARD-LIKELY]
Internal watershed infarcts show a characteristic "rosary" or "string of pearls" pattern on DWI MRI in the centrum semiovale. This imaging sign is increasingly recognized as pathognomonic for hemodynamic compromise.

**3. Border Zone Infarcts Constitute ~10% of All Brain Infarcts** [BOARD-CORE]

**Verification**: Multiple concordant sources (Radiographics 2011, PMC 2023).

### GAP IDENTIFIED
The app mentions "string of pearls" appearance but does not emphasize the mechanistic distinction between external (embolic) and internal (hemodynamic) watershed infarcts. This is a testable distinction.

## TOPIC 4: CEREBRAL VENOUS SINUS THROMBOSIS (CVST)

### What the App Currently Covers
Nothing -- CVST is not covered in the vascular anatomy module.

### New Findings (2020-2026)

**1. AHA Scientific Statement 2024** [BOARD-LIKELY]
The AHA published a comprehensive scientific statement on CVT diagnosis and management (Stroke, DOI: 10.1161/STR.0000000000000456). Key points:
- MRI/MRV or CT/CTV are primary diagnostic modalities
- D-dimer cannot reliably rule out CVT
- LMWH preferred over IV heparin (easier, no lab monitoring)
- Anticoagulate even with intracranial hemorrhage present

**2. German S2k Guideline 2024** [BOARD-LIKELY]
Updated CVT guidelines published in Neurological Research and Practice (2024) emphasize new risk factor identification and emerging role of DOACs.

**3. VITT Recognition** [BOARD-EMERGING]
Vaccine-induced immune thrombotic thrombocytopenia (VITT) was recognized as a distinct entity causing CVT, associated with adenoviral vector COVID-19 vaccines. Presents with severe thrombocytopenia + thrombosis.

**Verification**: AHA statement, German guideline, and PMC 2025 review all concordant.

### GAP IDENTIFIED
CVST is entirely absent from the vascular anatomy module. For board preparation, residents should know: (1) diagnostic approach (CT/CTV or MRI/MRV), (2) anticoagulation even with hemorrhage, (3) LMWH preferred, (4) VITT as a cause. Consider adding a section or at least board pearls.

## TOPIC 5: COLLATERAL CIRCULATION AND STROKE OUTCOMES

### What the App Currently Covers
- Circle of Willis collateral function
- Brief mention of collateral compensation during carotid stenosis

### New Findings (2020-2026)

**1. Leptomeningeal Collaterals Regulate Reperfusion** [BOARD-LIKELY]
A landmark 2024 Neuron study (PMID 38412858) demonstrated that leptomeningeal collaterals actively regulate reperfusion and can rescue the brain from "futile recanalization." In patients with poor collaterals, rapid reperfusion after thrombectomy can paradoxically cause hemorrhagic transformation.

**2. Collateral Status as Independent Outcome Predictor** [BOARD-LIKELY]
A 2022 meta-analysis (PMID 36195701) confirmed that leptomeningeal collateral status is independently associated with: smaller infarct cores, larger penumbras, reduced hemorrhagic transformation, and better 90-day functional outcomes.

**3. Multiphase CTA Collateral Scoring** [BOARD-LIKELY]
Multiphase CTA (mCTA) is now a validated tool for collateral assessment in acute stroke (AJNR 2021). It scores collaterals 0-5 and is superior to single-phase CTA for outcome prediction and thrombectomy selection, particularly in the late window (6-24 hours).

**4. Communicating Arteries and Leptomeningeal Collaterals Act Synergistically** [BOARD-EMERGING]
A 2024 MDPI study showed that Circle of Willis communicating arteries and leptomeningeal collaterals have independent but synergistic effects on stroke outcomes.

**Verification**: Multiple concordant sources across Neuron, PubMed meta-analysis, and AJNR.

### GAP IDENTIFIED
The app lacks coverage of leptomeningeal collaterals as a distinct concept from the Circle of Willis. This is increasingly important for board questions about thrombectomy patient selection.

## TOPIC 6: CT/MR ANGIOGRAPHY ADVANCES

### What the App Currently Covers
No dedicated imaging section in the vascular anatomy module.

### New Findings (2020-2026)

**1. Current State of Acute Stroke Imaging (2025)** [BOARD-LIKELY]
A 2025 Neurological Sciences review summarized: CTA and CTP are integral for LVO detection and penumbra assessment. Automated AI systems are enhancing speed and accuracy.

**2. Multiphase CTA** [BOARD-LIKELY]
mCTA provides time-resolved collateral assessment without additional contrast -- superior to single-phase CTA. Now widely adopted for thrombectomy triage.

**3. Virtual Non-Contrast CT (VNCCT)** [BOARD-EMERGING]
Reconstructed from dual-energy CTA, VNCCT allows simultaneous vascular and parenchymal evaluation from a single scan, potentially eliminating the need for a separate NCCT.

**4. AI/ML Integration** [BOARD-EMERGING]
Machine learning tools for automated LVO detection, collateral scoring, and ASPECTS calculation are increasingly validated and deployed clinically.

**5. Direct-to-Angio Suite (DTAS)** [BOARD-EMERGING]
Some centers bypass CT entirely for suspected LVO, taking patients directly to the angiography suite with flat-panel CT for initial assessment.

**Verification**: Neurological Sciences 2025, AJNR 2021.

### GAP IDENTIFIED
The app has no vascular imaging section. Consider adding key imaging pearls: DWI sensitivity vs CT, CTA for LVO detection, mCTA for collateral scoring.

## TOPIC 7: CAROTID WEB AS STROKE ETIOLOGY

### What the App Currently Covers
Not covered.

### New Findings (2020-2026)

**1. Definition and Epidemiology** [BOARD-EMERGING]
Carotid web is a focal form of fibromuscular dysplasia creating a shelf-like intimal projection in the carotid bulb. Prevalence 8.9% in cryptogenic stroke vs 1.9% in controls. Predominates in young females without traditional risk factors.

**2. High Recurrence on Medical Therapy** [BOARD-EMERGING]
A Stroke (AHA) 2024 study found diagnostic delays remain common and increase recurrence risk. 56% of medically-managed symptomatic patients had recurrent stroke (median 12 months). 72% ultimately required carotid revascularization.

**3. Diagnosis** [BOARD-EMERGING]
CTA is the preferred noninvasive method with excellent correlation to DSA (kappa = 0.92). The characteristic finding is a thin shelf-like projection at the posterior wall of the carotid bulb.

**Verification**: Stroke (AHA) 2018 systematic review, ESO 2020, Stroke 2024.

### GAP IDENTIFIED
Carotid web is not in the app. For board purposes, residents should know it as an underrecognized cause of recurrent stroke in young patients. It is increasingly appearing in vascular neurology board content.

## TOPIC 8: INTRACRANIAL ATHEROSCLEROSIS -- POST-SAMMPRIS

### What the App Currently Covers
Not directly addressed in the vascular anatomy module.

### New Findings (2020-2026)

**1. DAPT Beyond 90 Days** [BOARD-LIKELY]
A 2020 analysis of SAMMPRIS data (PMC 7686095) showed that continuing clopidogrel beyond 90 days produced a 44% numerical reduction in primary endpoints (6.0% vs 10.8%), but this was not statistically significant. Current AHA guidelines specify DAPT for 90 days for severe symptomatic intracranial stenosis.

**2. Persistent Benefit of Medical Management** [BOARD-CORE]
The SAMMPRIS trial's long-term data (median follow-up 32.4 months) confirmed durable superiority of aggressive medical management over stenting.

**3. Next-Generation Stenting** [BOARD-EMERGING]
Despite SAMMPRIS results, research continues on improved stent designs and patient selection criteria for cases refractory to medical therapy.

**Verification**: PMC 2020, NEJM 2011, Lancet 2014.

### GAP IDENTIFIED
SAMMPRIS and intracranial atherosclerosis management should be cross-referenced in the vascular anatomy module since it directly relates to intracranial vessel disease.

## TOPIC 9: BASILAR ARTERY OCCLUSION -- THROMBECTOMY TRIALS

### What the App Currently Covers
Basilar artery occlusion and locked-in syndrome mentioned briefly.

### New Findings (2020-2026)

**1. ATTENTION Trial (NEJM 2022)** [BOARD-LIKELY]
First positive RCT for basilar artery thrombectomy. Within 12 hours of onset: significantly higher good outcomes (mRS 0-3), mortality 37% vs 55%. Benefits sustained at 1 year.

**2. BAOCHE Trial (NEJM 2022)** [BOARD-LIKELY]
Extended the thrombectomy window to 6-24 hours for basilar occlusion, showing benefit in the late window.

**3. Context from Earlier Negative Trials** [BOARD-LIKELY]
BEST and BASICS trials were neutral but had significant methodological limitations (crossovers in BEST, enrollment issues in BASICS).

**4. Secondary Analyses** [BOARD-EMERGING]
2024 secondary analyses of ATTENTION data compared thrombectomy alone vs combined thrombolysis+thrombectomy, providing nuanced treatment guidance.

**Verification**: Both trials published in NEJM with rigorous peer review.

### GAP IDENTIFIED
The app should be updated to reflect that thrombectomy is now evidence-based for basilar occlusion (both early and late windows), representing a major practice change since 2022.

## TOPIC 10: VERTEBROBASILAR INSUFFICIENCY UPDATES

### What the App Currently Covers
- Vertebrobasilar anatomy
- VBI symptoms and syndromes
- Distinction from anterior circulation

### New Findings (2020-2026)

**1. VERiTAS Study (Stroke 2020)** [BOARD-LIKELY]
Demonstrated that hemodynamic status in vertebrobasilar disease can improve or worsen over time, and that flow status (both baseline and changes) independently predicts subsequent stroke risk.

**2. Posterior Circulation Represents ~20% of All Strokes** [BOARD-CORE]
StatPearls 2025 update confirms posterior circulation strokes are disproportionately morbid and mortal due to delayed diagnosis.

**3. Endovascular Treatment Now Evidence-Based** [BOARD-LIKELY]
The ATTENTION and BAOCHE trials have fundamentally changed management of acute basilar occlusion.

**Verification**: Stroke (AHA) 2020, StatPearls 2025.

### GAP IDENTIFIED
VERiTAS hemodynamic findings could be added as a pearl about prognostication in vertebrobasilar disease.

## TOPIC 11: FETAL PCA -- CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE UPDATE

### What the App Currently Covers
- Fetal PCA listed in variants table (15-30% prevalence)
- Clinical implication: PCA territory depends on anterior circulation

### New Findings (2020-2026)

**1. Fetal PCA as Risk Factor for Basilar Atherosclerosis** [BOARD-EMERGING]
A 2025 Frontiers in Neurology study found that fetal PCA is a morphological risk factor for basilar artery plaque formation (OR 3.196). The mechanism: reduced basilar flow in fetal PCA leads to altered hemodynamics promoting atherosclerosis.

**2. Hemodynamic Modeling** [BOARD-EMERGING]
A 2024 Physics of Fluids study used computational fluid dynamics to demonstrate asymmetric perfusion with unilateral fetal PCA, potentially heightening ischemic risk in specific territories.

**Verification**: Two independent 2024-2025 studies with complementary methodologies.

### GAP IDENTIFIED
Update the app's fetal PCA entry to note the emerging evidence that it promotes basilar atherosclerosis via hemodynamic changes.
