## 1. TENECTEPLASE: THE NEW STANDARD [BOARD-LIKELY]

### What Changed
Tenecteplase received FDA approval for acute ischemic stroke in March 2025, becoming the first new stroke thrombolytic approved in nearly 30 years. The 2026 AHA/ASA guidelines now list tenecteplase alongside alteplase as acceptable IV thrombolytic agents.

### Key Evidence
| Trial | Year | N | Key Finding |
|-------|------|---|-------------|
| AcT | 2024 | 1,600 | Non-inferiority of TNK vs alteplase confirmed |
| ATTEST-2 | 2024 | ~1,870 | TNK 0.25 mg/kg non-inferior to alteplase within 4.5h |
| ORIGINAL | 2024 | 1,430 | Non-inferiority confirmed in Chinese population |

### Clinical Advantages
- Single 5-second IV bolus (vs. 60-minute alteplase infusion)
- Higher fibrin specificity
- Longer half-life
- Easier prehospital/field administration
- Lower cost per dose

### Board Relevance
Expect questions on: advantages of tenecteplase over alteplase (single bolus vs infusion), dose (0.25 mg/kg), and the shift toward tenecteplase as first-line. The 2026 guidelines make this actionable knowledge.

### Sources
- Genentech FDA approval press release, March 2025
- ATTEST-2: Lancet Neurology 2024; S1474-4422(24)00377-6
- ORIGINAL: JAMA 2024; doi:10.1001/jama.2024.16035
- 2026 AHA/ASA Guideline: Stroke 2026; DOI:10.1161/STR.0000000000000513

## 2. 2026 AHA/ASA ACUTE ISCHEMIC STROKE GUIDELINES [BOARD-LIKELY]

### Major Changes from 2018/2019 Guidelines

**New Class 1 Recommendations:**

1. **Mobile Stroke Units:** MSUs for patients eligible for thrombolytics improve functional outcomes (B_PROUD, BEST-MSU trials)

2. **Extended Anterior Thrombectomy:** Thrombectomy for anterior LVO 6-24h: age <80, NIHSS >=6, ASPECTS 3-5 without mass effect -- lower ASPECTS threshold than previous guidelines

3. **Posterior Circulation Thrombectomy:** Basilar artery occlusion with NIHSS >=10 and mild ischemic damage within 24h -- FIRST TIME posterior circulation thrombectomy receives Class 1

4. **Tenecteplase:** Now listed as acceptable alternative to alteplase

5. **Dysphagia and Hyperglycemia:** Updated management protocols

6. **Pediatric Stroke:** New focused section (first time)

### Sources
- 2026 AHA/ASA Guideline: Stroke 2026; DOI:10.1161/STR.0000000000000513
- NeurologyLive analysis, 2026

## 3. MECHANICAL THROMBECTOMY EXPANSION [BOARD-CORE to BOARD-LIKELY]

### Anterior Circulation (Established = BOARD-CORE)
- 0-6h: Class 1, Level A (MR CLEAN, ESCAPE, EXTEND-IA, SWIFT PRIME, REVASCAT)
- 6-24h: Class 1 with imaging selection (DAWN, DEFUSE-3)
- DAWN: functional independence 49% vs 13% (control); NNT = 2.8
- DEFUSE-3: good outcome 45% vs 17%; NNT = 3.6

### Posterior Circulation (New = BOARD-LIKELY)
- BAOCHE (NEJM 2022): basilar occlusion 6-24h; good outcome 46% vs 23%
- ATTENTION (NEJM 2022): basilar occlusion within 12h; similar benefit
- 1-year ATTENTION follow-up (2024): benefit sustained; excellent outcome (mRS 0-1) increased from 20% to 28% in thrombectomy group -- benefits GROW over time
- Now Class 1 in 2026 AHA/ASA guidelines for basilar occlusion with NIHSS >=10

### Paradigm Shift: Tissue Clock
- Moving from fixed time windows to imaging-guided patient selection
- Individual infarct growth rate varies based on collateral circulation
- Some patients may benefit beyond 24h with favorable perfusion imaging
- ASPECTS and CTP mismatch are key selection tools

### Sources
- DAWN: Nogueira et al., NEJM 2018
- DEFUSE-3: Albers et al., NEJM 2018
- BAOCHE: Tao et al., NEJM 2022
- ATTENTION: Jovin et al., NEJM 2022
- 1-year ATTENTION follow-up: JAMA 2024

## 4. DUAL ANTIPLATELET THERAPY (DAPT) [BOARD-CORE]

### Established Regimens

**Aspirin + Clopidogrel (Standard of Care):**
- CHANCE: ASA + clopidogrel within 24h x 21 days; 32% RRR recurrent stroke
- POINT: ASA + clopidogrel within 12h x 90 days; reduced ischemic events but increased hemorrhage after 21 days
- Guideline: DAPT x 21 days then single antiplatelet

**Aspirin + Ticagrelor:**
- THALES (NEJM 2020): 11,016 patients; stroke/death 5.5% vs 6.6% (HR 0.83, p=0.02); severe bleeding 0.5% vs 0.1% (p=0.0001)
- Alternative to clopidogrel but with higher bleeding risk

### Pharmacogenomics Update [BOARD-LIKELY]
- CHANCE-2 (Lancet 2022): 6,412 CYP2C19 loss-of-function carriers
- Ticagrelor-aspirin: 33% relative risk reduction vs clopidogrel-aspirin
- Genotype-guided DAPT: test CYP2C19 --> poor metabolizers get ticagrelor instead of clopidogrel
- Subanalysis (2024): benefit seen in patients WITHOUT carotid stenosis; less effective with symptomatic carotid stenosis

### Board-Testable Points
- Start DAPT within 24h of minor stroke/high-risk TIA
- Duration: 21 days (not 90 -- bleeding risk increases)
- CYP2C19 poor metabolizers: switch to ticagrelor
- DAPT is for NON-CARDIOEMBOLIC stroke only

### Sources
- CHANCE: Wang et al., NEJM 2013
- POINT: Johnston et al., NEJM 2018
- THALES: Johnston et al., NEJM 2020
- CHANCE-2: Wang et al., Lancet 2022
- CHANCE-2 carotid subanalysis: PubMed 2024; 39773886

## 5. PFO CLOSURE [BOARD-CORE]

### Established Evidence
| Trial | Year | Key Finding |
|-------|------|-------------|
| CLOSE | 2017 | PFO closure + antiplatelet superior to antiplatelet alone; HR 0.03 |
| RESPECT Extended | 2017 | PFO closure reduced recurrent stroke; HR 0.55 |
| DEFENSE-PFO | 2018 | Confirmed closure benefit |

### Current Practice
- Closure recommended: age 18-60, cryptogenic stroke, high-risk PFO (large shunt or ASA)
- Pascal Classification System guides selection
- Post-closure: antiplatelet therapy (not anticoagulation typically)
- Complication: new-onset AF after device implantation

### 2024 ESO Guidelines
- First comprehensive GRADE-based PFO guideline
- Reinforces closure in selected patients <60
- No recommendation for closure in patients >=60 (insufficient evidence)

### Emerging: Age >=60 [BOARD-EMERGING]
- 2025 observational data suggests closure may be safe in older patients
- No RCT evidence yet
- Active area of investigation

### Sources
- CLOSE: Mas et al., NEJM 2017
- RESPECT Extended: Saver et al., NEJM 2017
- 2024 ESO PFO Guidelines: ESJ 2024; DOI:10.1177/23969873241247978
- Wang et al., ESJ 2025 (elderly PFO closure)

## 6. CRYPTOGENIC STROKE & CARDIAC MONITORING [BOARD-CORE]

### ILR AF Detection Rates (Cumulative)
| Timepoint | AF Detection Rate |
|-----------|------------------|
| 3 months | 12.2% |
| 6 months | 16.0% |
| 12 months | 18.7% |
| 24 months | 22.8% |
| 36 months | 28.5% |

### ILR vs External Monitoring
- ILR: higher AF detection and OAC initiation rates
- Cost: ~$13,000 additional at baseline + $2,500/year follow-up
- Similar 12-month mortality regardless of monitoring strategy
- Wearable devices (smartwatches, patches): comparable AF detection at lower cost

### Clinical Controversy
- Higher AF detection rates have NOT translated to lower stroke recurrence rates
- Question: Is detected asymptomatic AF the CAUSE of the index stroke or an incidental finding?
- This challenges the assumption that all cryptogenic strokes are occult AF

### Predictors of AF on ILR
- Older age, higher CHA2DS2-VASc, LA enlargement, prolonged P wave/PR interval

### Sources
- CRYSTAL-AF: Sanna et al., NEJM 2014
- Reveal LINQ Registry: JAHA 2024
- Wearable technology review: PubMed 2024; 38449074

## 7. LARGE VESSEL OCCLUSION DETECTION [BOARD-LIKELY]

### Prehospital Scale Comparison (2025 Pooled Analysis, n=2,358)
| Scale | AUROC | Best Feature |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| RACE | 0.79-0.81 | Highest overall clinical benefit |
| LAMS | 0.79-0.81 | Simplest (3 items) |
| G-FAST | 0.79-0.81 | Incorporates gaze |
| mG-FAST | 0.79-0.81 | Modified with gaze |
| CPSS + gaze | Lower | 94% specificity but only 35% sensitivity |

### Key Limitation
- All scales miss a significant proportion of LVOs
- Clinical scores CANNOT replace vessel imaging (CTA)
- Scales best used for field triage: direct suspected LVO to thrombectomy-capable center

### Board Relevance
- Know that RACE and LAMS are top-performing prehospital LVO scales
- Understand these guide transport decisions, not treatment decisions
- CTA remains gold standard for LVO diagnosis

### Sources
- 2025 Neurology pooled analysis: DOI:10.1212/WNL.0000000000213570
- RACE validation: PubMed 2024; 39719132

## 8. STROKE MIMICS [BOARD-CORE]

### Prevalence
20-40% of emergency stroke code activations are ultimately stroke mimics.

### Common Mimics by Frequency
1. Seizures (27% of neurological mimics)
2. Migraine with aura
3. Hypoglycemia (CHECK GLUCOSE FIRST -- readily correctable)
4. Functional neurological disorder (12.8% of all mimics)
5. Brain tumors
6. Peripheral vertigo
7. Toxic-metabolic encephalopathy

### Differentiation Clues
| Favors Mimic | Favors Stroke |
|-------------|--------------|
| Younger age | Older age |
| Dizziness, AMS | Dysarthria, hemiparesis |
| Seizure-like movements | Facial droop |
| Memory disturbance | Sudden onset, vascular territory |
| No vascular risk factors | AF, HTN, DM, smoking |

### Stroke Mimics Score (SMS) -- 2025
- New triage prediction tool developed for ED use
- Integrates clinical features to predict mimic vs true stroke
- Still requires validation

### Board Relevance
- Always check glucose before assuming stroke
- Risk of tPA for mimics is low but not zero (0.5% sICH)
- Seizure is the most common mimic presenting as stroke code

### Sources
- StatPearls Stroke Mimics (updated 2024)
- SMS development study: PubMed 2025; 40375550
- 2024 Lebanon ED study on mimic incidence

## 9. CAROTID INTERVENTION [BOARD-LIKELY]

### CREST-2 Trial (2025) -- Landmark Results

**Two Parallel Trials for Asymptomatic Carotid Stenosis:**

| Comparison | Primary Outcome | Significance |
|-----------|----------------|-------------|
| CAS + medical therapy vs medical alone | 2.8% vs 6.0% | SIGNIFICANT |
| CEA + medical therapy vs medical alone | 3.7% vs 5.3% | NOT significant |

### Clinical Implications
- First RCT showing CAS superiority over medical therapy alone in asymptomatic stenosis
- CEA did NOT reach statistical significance over medical therapy alone
- This challenges CEA's traditional dominance for asymptomatic disease
- Intensive medical therapy alone is now a viable option

### Current Recommendations Summary
| Indication | Intervention | Evidence |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Symptomatic >=70% | CEA or CAS | Class 1 |
| Symptomatic 50-69% | CEA may be considered | Class 2a |
| Asymptomatic >=60-70% | Consider if periop risk <3%, life expectancy >3-5y | Updated by CREST-2 |
| Age >70 | CEA preferred (lower periprocedural risk) | CREST original |
| Age <70 | CAS equivalent to CEA | CREST original |

### TCAR (Transcarotid Artery Revascularization) [BOARD-EMERGING]
- Hybrid approach: direct carotid access with flow reversal
- Potentially lower embolic risk than transfemoral CAS
- Gaining adoption but limited RCT data

### Sources
- CREST-2: 2025 results; tctmd.com analysis
- SVS 2022 Clinical Practice Guidelines
- ESVS 2023 Carotid Guidelines

## 10. NEUROPROTECTION [BOARD-EMERGING]

### Nerinetide (NA-1)
- Mechanism: PSD-95 inhibitor blocking NMDA-mediated excitotoxicity
- ESCAPE-NA1 (2020): overall neutral; tPA degrades nerinetide via plasmin
- ESCAPE-NEXT (2025): neutral overall; possible benefit within 3h without tPA
- FRONTIER (2025): tPA/EVT + nerinetide improved 90-day functional outcome vs placebo
- Key lesson: drug-drug interaction with tPA must be accounted for

### Other Emerging Agents
| Agent | Mechanism | Status |
|-------|----------|--------|
| Edaravone | Free radical scavenger | Approved in Japan; limited Western data |
| Uric acid | Endogenous antioxidant | URICO-ICTUS: trend toward benefit with alteplase |
| Scp776 | IGF-1-based | Promising Phase 2 (ARPEGGIO) |
| Remote ischemic conditioning | Endogenous protective pathways | Active trials |
| Normobaric hyperoxia | Oxygen supplementation | Under investigation |

### Board Relevance
- No neuroprotective agent has definitive clinical benefit for AIS (commonly tested fact)
- Control of BP, temperature, and glucose remains the best "neuroprotective" strategy
- Nerinetide story illustrates pharmacokinetic complexity in acute stroke

### Sources
- ESCAPE-NA1: Hill et al., Lancet 2020
- ESCAPE-NEXT & FRONTIER: 2025 presentations
- Neuroprotection review: Brain Circulation 2024
- ARPEGGIO Phase 2: Endovascular Today 2025
