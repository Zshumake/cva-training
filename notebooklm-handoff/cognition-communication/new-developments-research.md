## TOPIC 1: APHASIA CLASSIFICATION UPDATES

### Board-Readiness: BOARD-LIKELY

**What changed:** The dual-stream model (Hickok & Poeppel) has gained substantial traction as a complement to the classical Geschwind model. The dorsal stream (arcuate fasciculus pathway) handles phonological processing and repetition. The ventral stream (temporal lobe pathway to anterior temporal lobe) handles semantic processing. This explains why some patients have semantic comprehension deficits without classical Wernicke lesions.

**Board relevance:** The classical 8-type aphasia classification remains the primary board framework. The dual-stream model is increasingly referenced in newer PM&R texts. Residents should understand that classical categories have limitations -- not all patients fit neatly, and white matter tract damage (not just cortical lesions) plays a critical role.

**Gap in app content:** The app covers the classical classification comprehensively. Consider adding a brief note about the dual-stream model as supplementary context.

**Sources:**
- Neurology Perspectives 2022: Ventral language stream implications
- StatPearls Aphasia (updated 2024)
- Enhancing Aphasia Classification: PMC 2022

## TOPIC 2: CONSTRAINT-INDUCED LANGUAGE THERAPY (CILT/CIAT)

### Board-Readiness: BOARD-LIKELY

**What changed:** Multiple systematic reviews (2020-2023) now suggest that the constraint component of CILT may NOT be the active ingredient. When CILT was compared to equally intensive multimodality treatments, outcomes were similar. A 2020 systematic review of 12 RCTs showed significant improvement on WAB but not BNT or AAT. A 2023 umbrella review confirmed that CILT effects generally did not surpass comparison treatments.

**Key takeaway:** Intensity of therapy appears to be the critical factor. Approximately 14 hours of therapy over 2 weeks in individual sessions yielded outcomes equivalent to 30 hours of CILT group therapy.

**Gap in app content:** The app mentions CILT as "force verbal output" but does not address the intensity-vs-constraint debate. This is a clinically important distinction for board preparation.

**Sources:**
- Int J Rehab Research 2020: Systematic review of 12 RCTs
- J Speech Language Hearing Research 2023: Umbrella review of meta-analyses
- PLOS ONE 2017: Meta-analysis of RCTs

## TOPIC 3: MELODIC INTONATION THERAPY (MIT) -- NEURAL MECHANISMS

### Board-Readiness: BOARD-LIKELY

**What changed:** DTI studies show MIT produces structural changes in right hemisphere white matter (increased fractional anisotropy and fiber count). However, neuroimaging now shows MIT promotes bilateral neuroplastic changes, not purely right-hemisphere recruitment as originally theorized. A 2022 multi-level meta-analysis found small-to-moderate effects on language expression. A 2025 systematic review of 10 RCTs confirmed benefits for repetition and naming.

**Gap in app content:** The app correctly states MIT uses singing to access right hemisphere and is most effective for nonfluent aphasia with intact comprehension. The bilateral plasticity finding could be added as supplementary information.

**Sources:**
- PMC 2022: Multi-level meta-analysis of RCTs
- iScience 2023: DTI findings pilot study
- NYAS 2025: Systematic review of 10 RCTs

## TOPIC 4: tDCS FOR APHASIA

### Board-Readiness: BOARD-EMERGING

**What changed:** A 2024 systematic review of 16 reviews (9 with meta-analyses) found consistent improvements in naming (especially anodal tDCS over left IFG), improvements in repetition and fluency, but NO effect on comprehension. Most benefit seen in chronic stroke. tDCS is an adjunct to speech therapy, not standalone. Clinical significance for functional recovery remains uncertain.

**Board relevance:** Unlikely to be a board question focus in current exam cycles but increasingly discussed in PM&R literature. Residents should know the concept and limitations.

**Gap in app content:** The app does not cover tDCS for aphasia. The Cuccurullo text mentions tDCS briefly with inconclusive evidence (Elsner et al., 2015-2016 Cochrane reviews). More recent evidence is slightly more positive for naming outcomes specifically.

**Sources:**
- AJSLP 2024: Systematic review of 16 reviews
- Stroke 2023: RCT in subacute aphasia
- Frontiers Human Neuroscience 2021: Review

## TOPIC 5: HEMISPATIAL NEGLECT -- PRISM ADAPTATION

### Board-Readiness: BOARD-LIKELY

**What changed:** Two systematic reviews/meta-analyses (2021, 2025) provide moderate evidence for prism adaptation efficacy. The 2021 review (8 RCTs, 244 patients) showed significant improvement on BIT and Star Cancellation. The 2025 review (7 RCTs, 227 patients) confirmed short-term effects on neglect assessments but NOT on the Catherine Bergego Scale (functional neglect measure). Larger prism angles (>10 degrees) are more effective. Benefits persist at 1 month.

**Board relevance:** Prism adaptation is a testable concept. Residents should know the mechanism (rightward visual shift inducing leftward aftereffect) and that evidence supports short-term improvement on standardized tests.

**Gap in app content:** The app does not specifically mention prism adaptation. Consider adding it to the neglect treatment section.

**Sources:**
- AJPMR 2021: Systematic review of 8 RCTs
- Journal of Rehabilitation Medicine 2025: Meta-analysis of 7 RCTs

## TOPIC 6: NON-INVASIVE BRAIN STIMULATION FOR NEGLECT

### Board-Readiness: BOARD-EMERGING

**What changed:** Continuous theta-burst stimulation (cTBS) over contralesional posterior parietal cortex shows promise. Two weeks of cTBS improved BIT scores by 16.3% and 22.6% at follow-up. Also reduces ADL disability. Optokinetic stimulation activates spared frontoparietal regions but a recent RCT did not show clinically meaningful neglect remission.

**Board relevance:** Too new for current board cycles. Know the concept of interhemispheric inhibition balance.

**Gap in app content:** Not covered in current app. Appropriate as BOARD-EMERGING notation.

**Sources:**
- Neurology 2012: cTBS study
- ScienceDirect 2022: OKS + reading therapy RCT

## TOPIC 7: POST-STROKE COGNITIVE IMPAIRMENT -- PREVALENCE AND SCREENING

### Board-Readiness: BOARD-CORE (MoCA superiority) / BOARD-LIKELY (updated prevalence)

**What changed:** Updated prevalence data (2022-2025) consistently shows 65-77% of stroke survivors meet PSCI criteria. A landmark 2025 comparative meta-analysis (J Neurol) confirms MoCA is superior to MMSE for PSCI detection. Important nuance: age- and education-specific MoCA cutoffs for PSCI are NOT yet established, which limits valid application. Short-form MoCA is practical for acute stroke units.

**Gap in app content:** The app correctly identifies MoCA as preferred (this is well-covered). Consider adding the limitation about unstandardized cutoffs.

**Sources:**
- J Neurology 2025: Comparative meta-analysis MoCA vs MMSE
- Aging Clinical Experimental Research 2022: Normative datasets comparison
- Scientific Reports 2024: Improving MoCA diagnostic accuracy

## TOPIC 8: VASCULAR DEMENTIA DIAGNOSTIC CRITERIA

### Board-Readiness: BOARD-LIKELY

**What changed:** The **VasCog-2-WSO criteria** (2025, JAMA Neurology) provide an updated international standard for vascular cognitive impairment and dementia diagnosis. Key updates: operationalized criteria with guidance on neuroimaging and fluid biomarkers, two certainty levels (probable/possible), single cognitive domain impairment sufficient for diagnosis. DSM-5-TR uses "major/minor vascular neurocognitive disorder" terminology.

**Board relevance:** The Hachinski Ischemic Score and the distinction between vascular and Alzheimer dementia remain core board topics. The updated criteria terminology is BOARD-LIKELY.

**Gap in app content:** The app covers vascular dementia well (subtypes, Hachinski score, risk factors). Consider adding DSM-5-TR terminology update.

**Sources:**
- JAMA Neurology 2025: VasCog-2-WSO criteria
- PMC 2024: AHA/ASA vs DSM-5 diagnostic comparison

## TOPIC 9: APRAXIA REHABILITATION

### Board-Readiness: BOARD-LIKELY

**What changed:** A 2022 meta-analysis of RCTs found gesture training and strategy training had statistically significant effects on ADL performance in limb apraxia. Gesture training was effective for ideomotor apraxia post-treatment, though benefits may not persist at follow-up. A 2023 study (Stroke journal) highlighted that limb apraxias influence motor recovery independent of paresis -- higher-order perceptual and semantic deficits compound the motor impairment.

**Gap in app content:** The app covers apraxia classification well but does not address rehabilitation approaches. Consider adding gesture/strategy training.

**Sources:**
- ScienceDirect 2022: Meta-analysis of apraxia RCTs
- Stroke 2023: Limb apraxias and motor recovery

## TOPIC 10: PSEUDOBULBAR AFFECT AND EMOTIONAL DISORDERS

### Board-Readiness: BOARD-CORE

**What changed:** The PRISM II trial (PM&R 2019) specifically evaluated DM/Q in stroke patients (8.3% of 553 enrolled patients had stroke). Safety profile was generally acceptable. No major new developments in PBA pharmacotherapy since Nuedexta FDA approval in 2010. SSRIs remain the lower-cost alternative.

## TOPIC 11: APROSODIA / EMOTIONAL PROSODY

### Board-Readiness: BOARD-LIKELY (updated prevalence)

**What changed:** New research quantifies prevalence: approximately 70% of acute right hemisphere stroke patients have receptive emotional aprosodia, declining to 12-44% in subacute/chronic stages. White matter damage contributes significantly. Right frontal lesions correlate with expressive aprosodia; right temporal lesions with receptive aprosodia. Spousal impact data shows increased caregiver depression and decreased marital satisfaction.

**Gap in app content:** The app mentions aprosodia briefly under Right Hemisphere Cognitive Syndrome as "flat affect or aprosodia." Consider expanding with prevalence data and the expressive vs receptive distinction.

**Sources:**
- ScienceDirect 2021: Systematic review of lesion loci
- PMC 2022: Co-occurring deficits systematic review
- PMC 2020: Right hemisphere ventral stream evidence

## TOPIC 12: EXECUTIVE DYSFUNCTION REHABILITATION

### Board-Readiness: BOARD-LIKELY

**What changed:** Approximately 75% of stroke survivors have executive dysfunction. A 2022 Stroke journal systematic review and meta-analysis of RCTs supports metacognitive/compensatory strategy training as the evidence-based approach. Computerized cognitive training (CCT) shows moderate effects. New evidence (2025) includes:
- AI-driven telerehabilitation delivering comparable outcomes to therapist-supervised rehab
- Aerobic exercise enhancing left dorsolateral prefrontal cortex activation
- Combined cognitive + physical training approaches

**Gap in app content:** Not specifically addressed in the app. Executive dysfunction is mentioned in the cognitive domains table but not treatment approaches. Consider adding.

**Sources:**
- Stroke 2022: Systematic review and meta-analysis of cognitive rehab RCTs

## TOPIC 13: POST-STROKE DEPRESSION -- PREVENTION UPDATE

### Board-Readiness: BOARD-LIKELY

**What changed:** The AFFINITY trial (2021) was a major negative trial: fluoxetine 20mg for 26 weeks did NOT prevent PSD. This is important because earlier smaller trials suggested preventive SSRIs might work. Current consensus: prophylactic antidepressant use is NOT supported. Escitalopram may be effective for treatment (2022 meta-analysis, 7 RCTs). The AHA/ASA has not updated its 2016 PSD scientific statement.

**Gap in app content:** The app correctly states SSRIs are first-line for treatment. The prevention angle (AFFINITY trial negative) is not covered and is board-relevant.

**Sources:**
- AFFINITY trial 2021
- JAHA 2022: SSRI meta-analysis for poststroke recovery
- AHA Scientific Statement 2016
