## TOPIC 1: FIM and Outcome Measurement Updates

### Section GG Replacing FIM (BOARD-LIKELY)
- CMS mandated Section GG beginning October 2016; officially replaced FIM functional items October 2019 under the IMPACT Act
- Section GG self-care and mobility scores have strong positive correlations with FIM scores (r=0.87-0.95)
- Data collection from Section GG codes began October 2023, calculating new discharge scores including self-care and mobility
- Source: PMC 2022 (PMID: 36098957); CMS Federal Register August 2024
- Verification: Confirmed by CMS.gov IRF QRP documentation

### FIM Prediction Model Update (BOARD-LIKELY)
- 2024 study: B-ADL model (n=566 stroke patients) predicting motor FIM >61 at 90 days
- Variables: postural balance, albumin, age, arm function, days since onset, ADL level
- Source: ScienceDirect 2024 (Clinical Neurology and Neurosurgery)
- Verification: Published in peer-reviewed journal, consistent with prior FIM prediction literature

### Machine Learning for Outcome Prediction (BOARD-EMERGING)
- 2025 study validated NIHSS, age, and admission FIM as top predictors using ML methods
- Source: Scientific Reports 2025
- Verification: Consistent with established predictive variables; ML approach is novel application

## TOPIC 2: Modified Rankin Scale Updates

### Utility-Weighted mRS (BOARD-LIKELY)
- UW-mRS developed to incorporate health utility as a patient-centered outcome
- Addresses limitation that standard mRS does not reflect quality of life impact
- 2025 study examined population-specific utility values
- Source: Frontiers in Neurology 2025 (doi: 10.3389/fneur.2025.1539107)
- Verification: Builds on established UW-mRS methodology; growing adoption in trials

### mRS Reliability Improvements (BOARD-LIKELY)
- Structured mRS interview improves inter-rater reliability
- 2023 study across stroke units and rehabilitation wards demonstrated variable reliability in clinical practice
- Centralized adjudication recommended for clinical trials (MOST Trial comparison, 2024)
- Sources: PMC 2023; AHA Stroke 2024
- Verification: Consistent concern in stroke trial methodology literature

### mRS + NIHSS in Readmission Prediction (BOARD-LIKELY)
- 2025 JMIR study: Both scales significantly improve 30-day non-elective readmission and mortality prediction models
- Recommendation: Routinely incorporate these scales into risk prediction
- Source: JMIR Medical Informatics 2025 (doi: 10.2196/69102)
- Verification: Peer-reviewed publication, aligns with clinical intuition

## TOPIC 3: NIHSS Prognostic Value — Recent Validation

### Established Prognostic Thresholds (BOARD-CORE, reconfirmed)
- NIHSS <5: 80% discharged home
- NIHSS 6-13: typically require inpatient rehabilitation
- NIHSS >14: frequently require long-term skilled care
- Each 1-point increase reduces odds of excellent outcome by 24% at 7 days, 17% at 3 months
- Sources: Physiopedia; AHA Stroke 2019; MDCalc
- Verification: Well-established across multiple large cohort studies

### ML Validation (BOARD-EMERGING)
- 2025 Scientific Reports study confirmed NIHSS among most important features in ML-based prediction of inpatient rehabilitation outcomes
- Source: Scientific Reports 2025 (doi: 10.1038/s41598-025-00781-1)
- Verification: Novel methodology confirming established findings

## TOPIC 4: Stroke Unit vs General Ward Outcomes

### Current Evidence Summary (BOARD-CORE)
- Stroke units consistently improve functional outcomes and reduce length of stay
- Mortality benefit is less conclusive: OR 0.84, 95% CI 0.70-1.00, P=0.05
- 2024 retrospective study: stroke unit admission associated with 43% reduction in prolonged hospitalization but no significant mortality difference (HR=0.90)
- Benefits likely related to prevention/earlier recognition of medical complications and earlier mobilization
- Sources: PMC 2013 (meta-analysis); PubMed 2024 (Brazilian study)
- Verification: Cochrane reviews consistently show benefits; mortality effect sensitive to study inclusion criteria

## TOPIC 5: Early Supported Discharge (ESD)

### Recent RCT Evidence (BOARD-LIKELY)
- 2025 Korean KOMPACT study: ESD feasible in mild-moderate stroke, similar clinical outcomes to conventional rehabilitation, greater improvement in depressive symptoms
- ESD reduces length of hospital stay without increasing healthcare costs
- Encourages family involvement and patient self-reliance
- Frontiers 2022 systematic review confirmed ESD reduces institutional stay
- Sources: ScienceDirect 2025; Frontiers in Neurology 2022; AHA Circ CVQO 2020
- Verification: Consistent with prior Cochrane ESD reviews; Korean study extends evidence to Asian populations

## TOPIC 6: Telerehabilitation for Stroke

### Systematic Review Evidence (BOARD-LIKELY)
- 2024 meta-analysis of RCTs: telerehabilitation is effective and acceptable substitute for conventional stroke rehabilitation
- Effective domains: upper extremity training, speech-language therapy, cognitive rehabilitation, caregiver education
- Non-inferior to in-person therapy for motor function, balance, ADL, quality of life
- Hybrid model optimal: monthly/quarterly in-person + 2-5 weekly telerehab sessions
- Sources: PubMed 2024 systematic review; Frontiers in Neurology 2023
- Verification: Multiple systematic reviews converge on non-inferiority conclusion

### AI-Driven Telerehabilitation (BOARD-EMERGING)
- 2025 Frontiers RCT: AI-driven cognitive telerehabilitation effective for subacute stroke with cognitive impairment
- Compared self-guided AI to therapist-supervised rehabilitation
- Source: Frontiers in Neurology 2025 (doi: 10.3389/fneur.2025.1636017)
- Verification: Single RCT; promising but requires replication

### Remote Monitoring (BOARD-EMERGING)
- Wearable activity sensors, gait sensors for home exercise feedback
- Growing evidence base but not yet standard of care
- Source: Multiple Frontiers publications 2023-2025
- Verification: Technology rapidly advancing; clinical validation still in progress

## TOPIC 7: Driving After Stroke

### Assessment Framework (BOARD-CORE, reconfirmed)
- No universal timeline; 4 weeks minimum for mild stroke, 6 months in some jurisdictions
- Behind-the-wheel evaluation remains gold standard
- Screening tools: Compass test, Road Sign Recognition Test, Trail Making Test B, Rey-Osterrieth Complex Figure
- Sources: NCBI Bookshelf (Clinical Pathways in Stroke Rehabilitation) 2022; American Stroke Association
- Verification: No major guideline changes 2020-2025; state laws still variable

### Recurrence Risk During Driving Period (BOARD-LIKELY)
- 2023 PMC cohort study found stroke recurrence is a concern among those referred for driving assessment
- Reinforces need for adequate secondary prevention before return to driving
- Source: PMC 2023 (PMID: 36839555)
- Verification: Published in peer-reviewed journal; clinically relevant finding

## TOPIC 8: Return to Work After Stroke

### Updated Rates and Predictors (BOARD-CORE)
- Overall return to work: 25-50% (general); 68.8% in young adults 18-50 (2025 JAHA multicenter study)
- Positive predictors: ADL independence at 3 months, white-collar occupation, household income contribution, male gender
- Negative predictors: large-artery atherosclerosis, diabetes, NIHSS >15, hemorrhagic stroke, aphasia
- Sources: JAHA 2025 (doi: 10.1161/JAHA.124.036427); Archives PM&R 2023; ScienceDirect 2024
- Verification: Consistent across multiple systematic reviews and cohort studies

### Multi-Domain Predictive Framework (BOARD-LIKELY)
- 2023 Archives PM&R systematic review and meta-analysis organized predictors into three domains: physical (severity, disability), social (ethnicity, income, gender, occupation), cognitive/emotional (psychiatric disorders, fatigue, cognitive function)
- Source: Archives of Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation 2023
- Verification: Comprehensive meta-analysis, large evidence base

### COVID-19 Impact on Return to Work (BOARD-EMERGING)
- RETAKE trial (2025): COVID-19 pandemic significantly disrupted return-to-work outcomes
- Remote work options both helped (reduced transportation barriers) and hindered (reduced employer engagement)
- Source: Frontiers in Sociology 2025
- Verification: Qualitative study within established RCT framework

## TOPIC 9: Caregiver Burden

### Intervention Evidence (BOARD-LIKELY)
- 2021 AHA systematic review: 18 studies, 7 caregiver and 4 dyad interventions showed significant improvements
- Effective interventions: educational components, 7-9 sessions
- Dyadic interventions improved survivor physical functioning AND caregiver depression
- BUT: meta-analyses of overall burden reduction showed nonsignificant effects
- Source: Stroke AHA 2022 (doi: 10.1161/STROKEAHA.121.034090)
- Verification: AHA-published systematic review; high methodological quality

### Predictors of Caregiver Burden (BOARD-LIKELY)
- Key predictors: severity of stroke-related disability, caregiver physical health, social support availability, financial resources
- Structured educational programs are most consistently effective
- Caregiver preparedness is a critical determinant of successful home discharge
- Source: PMC 2025 (narrative review)
- Verification: Consistent with prior literature on caregiver outcomes

## TOPIC 10: Long-Term Stroke Outcomes (5-Year Survival and Recurrence)

### Updated Survival Data (BOARD-CORE)
- Overall 5-year survival: 50-59% (varies by sex and stroke subtype)
- Highest survival: small-artery occlusion (72-76%)
- Lowest survival: cardioembolic stroke (36-48%)
- By age: under 45 years 85-90%, over 75 years 55-65%
- Sources: Stroke AHA 2020 (doi: 10.1161/STROKEAHA.120.029972); PMC 2020
- Verification: Large population-based cohort studies

### Updated Recurrence Data (BOARD-CORE)
- 5-year recurrence rate: 12-20% (improving trend)
- Reduced from 18% (1995-1999) to 12% (2000-2005) with no further improvement since
- Annual risk of second stroke: approximately 5%
- Cumulative risk of recurrence or death by 5 years reduced from 65% (1995-1999) to 43% (2010-2015)
- Source: Aked et al. European Journal of Neurology 2021; AHA Stroke 2020
- Verification: Multiple population-based registries confirm improving trend

## TOPIC 11: Community Reintegration

### Rehabilitation Transition Programs (BOARD-LIKELY)
- 2024 JAMA Network Open RCT: structured rehabilitation transition programs significantly improve community participation
- Depression, fatigue, mobility, environmental support strongly associated with participation
- Sources: JAMA Network Open 2024; PMC 2022
- Verification: High-quality RCT published in major journal

### Assessment Tools Update (BOARD-CORE, reconfirmed)
- Reintegration to Normal Living Index (RNLI): 11 items, 10-point visual analog scale
- Community Integration Questionnaire (CIQ): home, social, and productive activities
- Community Participation Indicators (CPI)
- Source: Multiple publications
- Verification: Well-established measures

### Young Adult Stroke Community Reintegration (BOARD-EMERGING)
- Stroke in adults 18-64 is increasing significantly worldwide
- This population encounters particularly significant difficulties with social role reintegration
- Narrative and skills-building interventions being studied in RCTs
- Source: BMC Neurology 2021; PLOS One 2025
- Verification: Growing evidence base for age-specific interventions

## TOPIC 12: CMS Quality Metrics Updates

### Stroke-Specific Quality Measures (BOARD-LIKELY)
- Current CMS eCQMs aligned for 2025: STK-2 (Discharged on Antithrombotic), STK-3 (Anticoagulation for AF/Flutter), STK-5 (Antithrombotic by Hospital Day 2)
- For 2025: CMS finalized addition of 7 new quality measures and removal of 10 measures
- Source: CMS.gov 2024; Joint Commission 2025
- Verification: Official CMS documentation

### Upcoming Changes for 2026-2027 (BOARD-LIKELY)
- 30-Day Stroke Mortality Rate (MORT-30-STK): switching to 2-year data window from 3 years
- Hospital 30-Day RSMR for AIS: incorporating claims-based risk adjustment for stroke severity (beginning July 2023-June 2025 reporting period)
- Increases importance of granular ICD-10 documentation for comorbidities, complications, and stroke severity
- Source: CMS Federal Register 2024; Dexur analysis 2025
- Verification: Official CMS proposed and final rules

## GAP ANALYSIS: Content Gaps Identified in Current App Module

### Gaps Found (content NOT in the current app that should be added):

1. **Section GG replacing FIM** — The app still describes FIM as the primary measure without mentioning that CMS has replaced FIM with Section GG items in IRF reporting since October 2019. This is board-relevant.

2. **NIHSS prognostic thresholds** — The app mentions NIHSS measures neurological deficit but does not include the specific discharge prediction thresholds (<5 home, 6-13 IRF, >14 long-term care). These are high-yield board numbers.

3. **5-year survival stratified by stroke subtype** — The app states 25-30% recurrence within 5 years but lacks the survival data stratified by stroke subtype (small-artery best at 72-76%, cardioembolic worst at 36-48%).

4. **Utility-weighted mRS (UW-mRS)** — Not mentioned in the app. This is a growing concept in stroke trial design.

5. **ESD programs** — Early Supported Discharge is not covered. This is an important model of care in the continuum.

6. **IRF quality measures and IMPACT Act** — The app mentions Section GG briefly but does not explain the IMPACT Act mandate or current IRF quality metrics.

7. **Return-to-work multi-domain predictive framework** — The app lists predictors but does not organize them by domain (physical, social, cognitive/emotional) as recent systematic reviews do.

8. **RETAKE trial and pandemic effects on return to work** — Not mentioned.

9. **AI-driven telerehabilitation** — Not mentioned; emerging topic.

10. **2024 AHA/ASA Primary Prevention of Stroke Guideline** — Not referenced; major new guideline.
