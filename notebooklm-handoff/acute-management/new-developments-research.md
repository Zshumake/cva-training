# New Developments Research Report: Acute Stroke Management (2020-2026)
## CVA Training App -- PM&R Board Exam Preparation
## Research Date: 2026-03-21

---

## OVERVIEW

This report documents new developments in acute stroke management since 2020 that are relevant to PM&R board examination preparation. Each claim is source-tagged and classified by board-readiness level:

- **BOARD-CORE**: Established guidelines/trials that are definitely tested on current exams
- **BOARD-LIKELY**: Recent but adopted findings that are probably appearing on exams soon
- **BOARD-EMERGING**: Too new for current exam cycles; 2-3 years out

---

## 1. 2026 AHA/ASA ACUTE ISCHEMIC STROKE GUIDELINE [BOARD-LIKELY]

The 2026 Guideline for the Early Management of Patients With Acute Ischemic Stroke replaces the 2018 Guidelines and 2019 update. This is the most significant update in acute stroke management in 8 years.

### Top Changes:

1. **Tenecteplase endorsed alongside alteplase** (Class 1 recommendation) for IV thrombolysis within 4.5 hours. Either tenecteplase 0.25 mg/kg or alteplase 0.9 mg/kg is acceptable.

2. **Non-disabling deficits**: For patients with non-disabling deficits in the 4.5-hour window, thrombolysis is NOT recommended. Dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT) is preferred instead.

3. **Expanded thrombectomy eligibility**: Now formally includes patients with basilar artery occlusions and those with large ischemic cores.

4. **BP management after reperfusion**: Intensive SBP lowering to <140 mmHg is NOT recommended after thrombolysis or thrombectomy (may cause harm).

5. **Mobile stroke units**: Formally endorsed for rapid thrombolysis delivery.

6. **Dysphagia management**: Updated recommendations for more active dysphagia therapies.

7. **Pediatric stroke**: First detailed recommendations for diagnosing and treating stroke in children.

**Source**: AHA/ASA 2026 Guideline for the Early Management of Patients With Acute Ischemic Stroke, Stroke, published 2026. Available at: https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/STR.0000000000000513

### Gap vs Current App Content:

The app currently references "AHA/ASA 2024 guidelines" for tenecteplase and covers the 2018 guideline framework. The 2026 guideline introduces several changes the app should reflect, particularly the formal Class 1 recommendation for tenecteplase, the non-disabling deficit recommendation against thrombolysis, and the expanded thrombectomy eligibility criteria.

## 2. TENECTEPLASE VS ALTEPLASE: DEFINITIVE EVIDENCE [BOARD-LIKELY]

Multiple international RCTs published 2022-2024 have established tenecteplase as non-inferior to alteplase, leading to the 2026 guideline endorsement.

### Key Trials:

**AcT Trial (2022)**
- Canadian RCT, 1,600 patients
- Tenecteplase 0.25 mg/kg non-inferior to alteplase
- May have higher early recanalization rates for LVO
- Source: Lancet 2022

**TASTE Trial (2024)**
- Multicentre phase 3 non-inferiority trial
- Perfusion-imaging-selected patients within 4.5 hours
- Primary outcome: 59% tenecteplase vs 56% alteplase (per protocol)
- Non-inferiority confirmed
- Source: Lancet Neurology 2024

**ATTEST-2 Trial (2024)**
- Randomized, parallel group, open-label trial
- Tenecteplase 0.25 mg/kg non-inferior to alteplase 0.9 mg/kg within 4.5 hours
- Source: Lancet Neurology 2024

**ORIGINAL Trial (2024)**
- 1,465 patients randomized
- Primary outcome: 72.7% tenecteplase vs 70.3% alteplase (RR 1.03; non-inferiority met)
- Source: JAMA 2024

**Updated Meta-Analysis (2025)**
- Aggregate data from all RCTs suggest likely superiority of tenecteplase for excellent outcomes (mRS 0-1) at 90 days
- Real-world implementation data show reduced door-to-needle and door-to-puncture times with tenecteplase
- Eliminates need for infusion pumps and monitoring, reducing resource requirements
- Source: Multiple meta-analyses published 2024-2025

### Practical Advantages of Tenecteplase:

- Single IV bolus (5-10 seconds) vs 60-minute infusion
- No infusion pump needed
- Ideal for drip-and-ship transfers between hospitals
- Reduced door-to-needle times in real-world implementation
- Lower dose (0.25 mg/kg max 25 mg) vs alteplase (0.9 mg/kg max 90 mg)
- Similar hemorrhagic risk (~6% sICH)

### Gap vs Current App Content:

The app already includes a tenecteplase comparison section and MedicationCardBlock. The AcT trial reference is present. The app should be updated to reflect the 2026 Class 1 recommendation and add the TASTE, ATTEST-2, and ORIGINAL trial results.

## 3. LARGE CORE THROMBECTOMY TRIALS [BOARD-LIKELY]

Six RCTs published 2022-2023 have revolutionized thrombectomy eligibility by demonstrating benefit in patients with LARGE ischemic cores (ASPECTS 3-5), previously considered ineligible.

### Key Trials:

**RESCUE-Japan LIMIT (2022)**: Showed benefit of EVT for large anterior circulation infarctions with ASPECTS 3-5.

**ANGEL-ASPECT (2023)**: Thrombectomy improved 90-day functional outcomes in large cores up to 24 hours after onset.

**SELECT2 (2023)**: EVT + medical care superior to medical care alone in large core strokes up to 24 hours. NNT approximately 5. Sustained benefit confirmed at 1-year follow-up. Published in NEJM.

**TENSION (2023)**: EVT improved functional outcome, survival, and quality of life at 12 months in large vessel occlusions with large infarcts within 12 hours. 12-month follow-up published in Lancet Neurology 2024 confirmed sustained benefits.

**LASTE (2023)**: Additional confirmatory evidence from French trial.

**TESLA (2023)**: Included in meta-analyses supporting large core thrombectomy.

**AHA Science Advisory (2024)**: Formally endorsed thrombectomy for large core strokes based on this evidence base.

**Critical Appraisal (2025)**: Stroke journal published comprehensive appraisal of evidence from all 6 RCTs, confirming the treatment paradigm shift.

### Clinical Implication:

Patients with large ischemic cores (ASPECTS 3-5) who were previously excluded from thrombectomy are now eligible. The 2026 AHA/ASA guideline formally incorporates this expanded eligibility.

### Gap vs Current App Content:

The app does NOT include large core thrombectomy trials. The current thrombectomy eligibility criteria state "ASPECTS score >= 6." This needs to be updated to reflect that patients with ASPECTS 3-5 may now be eligible based on SELECT2, ANGEL-ASPECT, RESCUE-Japan LIMIT, and TENSION.

## 4. POSTERIOR CIRCULATION THROMBECTOMY [BOARD-LIKELY]

### Key Trials:

**ATTENTION Trial (2022)**
- Endovascular treatment for acute basilar artery occlusion
- 46% good functional status (mRS 0-3) vs 23% medical management
- Published in NEJM
- Source: NEJM 2022

**BAOCHE Trial (2022)**
- Thrombectomy 6-24 hours after basilar artery occlusion
- 46% vs 24% good functional status (P < 0.001)
- 90-day mortality: 37% thrombectomy vs 55% control
- Published in NEJM
- Source: NEJM 2022

**3-Year Follow-Up (2025)**
- 38.4% thrombectomy vs 18.3% medical management achieved ambulatory/self-care outcomes
- Benefit sustained long-term
- Source: Neurology Today 2025

**Meta-Analysis (BEST, BASICS, ATTENTION, BAOCHE)**
- Twofold increase in odds of favorable outcome with EVT for basilar artery occlusion
- Source: Published 2023

### 2026 Guideline Impact:

The 2026 AHA/ASA guideline now formally includes patients with basilar artery occlusions as eligible for thrombectomy. This represents a major expansion of thrombectomy indications.

### Gap vs Current App Content:

The app's thrombectomy section focuses exclusively on anterior circulation LVO. It does NOT mention posterior circulation thrombectomy, ATTENTION, or BAOCHE trials. This is a significant gap that should be addressed.

## 5. MEDIUM-VESSEL OCCLUSION (MeVO) THROMBECTOMY [BOARD-EMERGING]

### 2025 Trial Results:

Several 2025 trials examined thrombectomy for medium-vessel or distal occlusions:
- **ESCAPE-MeVO**: Did NOT show benefit over medical therapy
- **DISTAL**: Did NOT show benefit
- **DISCOUNT**: Did NOT show benefit; reported higher rates of intracranial hemorrhage

**Clinical implication**: Thrombectomy for distal/medium vessel occlusions beyond M1 is NOT currently recommended. This is an active area of research but has not yet demonstrated benefit.

## 6. BLOOD PRESSURE MANAGEMENT AFTER REPERFUSION [BOARD-LIKELY]

### ENCHANTED2/MT Trial

The ENCHANTED2/MT trial was a landmark study terminated early for safety concerns:
- Intensive BP lowering to SBP <120 mmHg after successful endovascular thrombectomy was HARMFUL
- More intensive BP lowering was associated with worse functional outcomes
- More early neurological deterioration in the intensive group
- The adverse effect emerged within 7 days

**Source**: Anderson et al., Lancet 2022 (published online November 2022)

### 2024-2025 Meta-Analyses

Multiple meta-analyses of RCTs (2024-2025) confirmed that more intensive blood pressure lowering after endovascular therapy does not improve outcomes and may cause harm.

### 2025 Blood Pressure Guidelines Comparison

A 2025 review compared BP management recommendations across AHA 2025, ESC 2024, ESH 2023, and JSH 2025 guidelines:
- ALL guidelines discourage routine BP lowering in acute ischemic stroke unless SBP >=220 or DBP >=120 mmHg
- ALL converge on pre-treatment BP <185/110 and maintenance <180/105 for 24 hours after reperfusion
- Intensive lowering to <120 mmHg is specifically NOT recommended

**Source**: PubMed 2025 (comparative review of guidelines)

### Gap vs Current App Content:

The app correctly states BP targets for various scenarios. The ENCHANTED2/MT trial findings should be added as a warning against overly aggressive BP lowering post-thrombectomy. The 2026 guideline's explicit recommendation AGAINST intensive SBP lowering to <140 mmHg after reperfusion should be incorporated.

## 7. EARLY MOBILIZATION: AVERT UPDATES [BOARD-CORE]

### AVERT Dose-Response Analysis

The AVERT dose-response analysis provided important nuance beyond the headline finding:
- Increased FREQUENCY of out-of-bed sessions improved outcomes
- Increased DURATION (minutes per day) of mobilization worsened outcomes
- Optimal approach: short, frequent mobilization sessions

**Source**: Bernhardt et al., Neurology 2016

### Current Practice (2024-2025)

- Most stroke centers now initiate passive bed exercise on day 1
- Head elevation on day 1
- Out-of-bed mobilization on day 2
- Avoid aggressive mobilization within first 24 hours

### AVERT-DOSE Trial

A follow-up trial (AVERT-DOSE) is investigating the optimal frequency and dose of early mobilization based on the dose-response analysis findings.

### 2024 VA/DoD Clinical Practice Guideline

Updated in July 2024, emphasizes early but not overly aggressive rehabilitation initiation. Increasing daily dose of rehabilitation significantly associated with improved ADL outcomes in all age groups.

**Source**: Annals of Internal Medicine 2025 (VA/DoD CPG synopsis)

### Gap vs Current App Content:

The app does not currently address early mobilization or the AVERT trial findings. This is important PM&R-specific content that should be added, as the optimal timing and dosing of mobilization is a key clinical question for physiatrists.

## 8. GLUCOSE MANAGEMENT: SHINE TRIAL FOLLOW-UP [BOARD-CORE]

### SHINE Trial Post-Thrombolysis Analysis (2024)

A prespecified secondary analysis from the SHINE trial published in 2024 found:
- Intensive insulin therapy was NOT associated with reduced risk of post-thrombolysis sICH compared with standard sliding scale
- Early post-thrombolysis hyperglycemia was independently associated with higher risk of sICH
- Risk was particularly elevated in older patients with more severe strokes

**Source**: Neurology 2024

### Clinical Implication:

While intensive glucose control (80-130 mg/dL) does not improve outcomes, persistent hyperglycemia after thrombolysis is independently dangerous. The standard target of 140-180 mg/dL remains appropriate.

### Gap vs Current App Content:

The app already covers the SHINE trial and glucose targets well. The 2024 post-thrombolysis analysis adds the important nuance about hyperglycemia being an independent risk factor for sICH after thrombolysis.

## 9. DVT PROPHYLAXIS UPDATES [BOARD-CORE]

### Current Evidence Summary:

The evidence base remains stable with the CLOTS 3 and PREVAIL trials forming the foundation:
- **IPC (CLOTS 3)**: Reduces DVT by 34%, improves survival in immobile patients
- **Enoxaparin (PREVAIL)**: Superior to UFH (40 mg daily vs 5000 U BID)
- **Graduated compression stockings**: NOT useful
- **ICH patients**: LMWH prophylaxis may start days 2-4 (reasonable over no prophylaxis)
- **Combination therapy**: In high-risk patients, combining IPC + LMWH reduced DVT from ~4% to ~1%

### Gap vs Current App Content:

The app briefly mentions DVT prophylaxis with IPC and "pharmacologic when safe." The specific trial evidence (CLOTS 3, PREVAIL), the enoxaparin superiority data, and the timing for ICH patients could be expanded.

## 10. TEMPERATURE MANAGEMENT [BOARD-CORE / BOARD-EMERGING]

### Established Practice [BOARD-CORE]:

- Fever is independently associated with worse outcomes
- Target normothermia (36.0-37.5 degrees C)
- Acetaminophen 650-1000 mg q4-6h; cooling devices for refractory fever
- Search for and treat underlying infection

### Therapeutic Hypothermia [BOARD-EMERGING]:

- Remains investigational for stroke (unlike post-cardiac arrest)
- COTTIS pilot study (2024) examined targeted temperature management combined with thrombectomy
- Mild hypothermia to 35 degrees C appears safe and feasible
- Definitive clinical benefit NOT yet established
- Controlled normothermia may achieve similar outcomes without sedation/anti-shivering agents

### Gap vs Current App Content:

The app covers temperature management well. No major updates needed beyond noting the ongoing investigational status of therapeutic hypothermia.

## 11. DECOMPRESSIVE CRANIECTOMY [BOARD-CORE]

### DESTINY II Long-Term Data:

A 2025 publication in Springer Nature reviewed DESTINY II outcomes in the context of current practice:
- Hemicraniectomy in patients >60 years significantly improved survival
- Most survivors had substantial disability (mRS 4-5)
- Mortality: 51.3% in >60 group vs 20.8% in <60 group
- Remains a shared decision-making conversation emphasizing quality of life expectations

**Source**: Springer Nature 2025 (Decompressive Craniectomy in Older Patients chapter)

### Gap vs Current App Content:

The app covers DESTINY II well with appropriate clinical context about shared decision-making. No significant gaps identified.

## 12. STROKE UNIT CARE [BOARD-CORE]

### Cochrane Network Meta-Analysis (2020, Updated):

Organized stroke unit care produces:
- Two extra survivors per 100 patients
- Six more patients living at home per 100 patients
- Six more patients living independently per 100 patients
- No increase in hospital length of stay
- Stroke ward care and mixed rehabilitation ward care show the most benefit
- Stroke team consultations (without dedicated unit) show little difference

**Source**: Cochrane Database Syst Rev 2020 (Langhorne et al.)

### Gap vs Current App Content:

The app does not currently include stroke unit care evidence. This is relevant for PM&R boards as it supports the role of organized multidisciplinary care in stroke outcomes.
