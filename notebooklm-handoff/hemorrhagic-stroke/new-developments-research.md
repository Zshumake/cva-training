## 1. 2022 AHA/ASA ICH Guidelines

**Source:** Greenberg SM, et al. Stroke. 2022;53:e282-e361. Published May 17, 2022.
**URL:** https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/STR.0000000000000407
**Board Tag:** BOARD-CORE

### What Changed from 2015 Guidelines:
1. **Blood pressure:** SBP target 140 mmHg with range 130-150 mmHg for patients presenting with SBP 150-220 mmHg. Initiate within 2 hours, reach target within 1 hour. (Class 2a recommendation)
2. **DVT prevention:** Compression stockings of ANY length have NO benefit. Intermittent pneumatic compression is recommended. (New)
3. **Anticoagulant reversal:** 4F-PCC recommended over FFP for warfarin-associated ICH. Idarucizumab for dabigatran. 4F-PCC or aPCC may be considered for factor Xa inhibitors.
4. **Seizures:** Routine prophylaxis NOT recommended. Treat clinical seizures. Continuous EEG reasonable if consciousness disproportionately altered.
5. **Surgery:** Cerebellar hemorrhage >3 cm with brainstem compression remains indication. No strong recommendation for supratentorial ICH surgery at time of publication.
6. **Goals of care:** Early care-limitation orders should be avoided. Full aggressive care recommended initially. ICH Score should not be used alone for withdrawal decisions.
7. **Platelet transfusion:** HARMFUL outside of emergency surgery or severe thrombocytopenia (PATCH trial evidence).
8. **Recombinant factor VIIa:** NOT recommended (reduces hematoma growth but no outcome benefit).

### Gaps in Current App Content:
- App mentions INTERACT2 trial correctly
- App correctly covers BP target of <140 mmHg
- App needs update: should specify SBP range 130-150 mmHg (not just "<140")
- App needs update: should emphasize initiation within 2 hours, target within 1 hour
- App should add: compression stockings ineffective for DVT prevention
- App should add: early care-limitation orders cautioned against

**Verification:** Cross-referenced with AHA professional.heart.org summary and emDocs guideline review.

## 2. INTERACT3 Trial (2023)

**Source:** Anderson CS, et al. The Lancet. 2023;401(10388):1603-1611.
**URL:** https://www.thelancet.com/article/S0140-6736(23)00806-1/fulltext
**Board Tag:** BOARD-LIKELY

### Key Findings:
- International stepped-wedge cluster randomized trial in 10 countries (primarily low/middle-income)
- Tested a goal-directed care BUNDLE: early intensive BP lowering (<140 mmHg target) + management algorithms for hyperglycemia, pyrexia, and abnormal anticoagulation
- The care bundle was safe and effective in improving functional outcomes
- Distinguishes from INTERACT2: this was a bundle approach, not BP lowering alone
- Supports the concept that a systematic, bundled approach to ICH management within hours of onset improves outcomes

### Board Relevance:
- Reinforces SBP <140 mmHg target as part of broader management
- May appear on boards as the trial that demonstrates bundled care superiority
- Builds on INTERACT2 evidence base

### App Impact:
- App currently references INTERACT2 only. Should note INTERACT3 supports bundled approach.

**Verification:** Confirmed through Lancet publication and PMC full text.

## 3. ATACH-2 Follow-up Analyses (2020-2021)

**Source:** Multiple secondary analyses of the ATACH-2 trial
**Board Tag:** BOARD-CORE (primary trial); BOARD-LIKELY (subgroup analyses)

### Key Secondary Findings:
1. **Moderate-to-severe ICH subgroup (2020):** Intensive BP lowering (110-139 mmHg) showed no benefit in sicker patients. Published in Cerebrovascular Diseases.
2. **Renal impairment subgroup (2021):** Significant interaction between eGFR and treatment -- safety concerns for intensive BP lowering in patients with renal impairment. Published in Neurology.
3. **DWI lesion analysis (2021):** No difference in diffusion-weighted imaging lesions between intensive and standard treatment groups (~25% in both).

### Board Relevance:
- Confirms that going below SBP 140 mmHg (to 110-139 mmHg range) provides NO additional benefit
- Important to distinguish from INTERACT2 which showed some benefit with ordinal analysis at <140 mmHg target
- Caution in patients with renal impairment receiving aggressive BP lowering

**Verification:** Confirmed through PubMed entries and original NEJM publication.

## 4. ENRICH Trial (NEJM 2024) -- LANDMARK

**Source:** Awad IA, et al. NEJM. 2024;390(14):1277-1289. Published April 2024.
**URL:** https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa2308440
**Board Tag:** BOARD-LIKELY

### Study Details:
- 300 patients across 37 US centers (2017-2023)
- Randomized to early (within 24 hours) minimally invasive parafascicular surgery (MIPS) using BrainPath device vs standard medical management
- Inclusion: spontaneous supratentorial ICH, hematoma 30-80 mL, both lobar (n=208) and anterior basal ganglia (n=92)

### Primary Results:
- Utility-weighted mRS at 180 days: 0.458 surgery vs 0.374 medical (difference 0.084)
- Posterior probability of superiority: 0.981
- First positive randomized trial for surgical ICH evacuation

### Secondary Results:
- 30-day survival: 90.7% surgery vs 82% medical
- ICU stay: 6.9 vs 9.7 days
- Hospital stay: 14.9 vs 18.1 days
- Need for decompressive craniectomy: 3.3% vs 20%
- Benefit driven primarily by LOBAR hemorrhages (utility-weighted mRS 0.51 vs 0.37)
- Cost-effective: $8,850 less per patient in surgery group

### Board Relevance:
- Game-changing trial for ICH management
- Likely to appear on future board exams
- Key teaching point: benefit was location-dependent (lobar > deep)
- Represents shift from decades of negative surgical trials

### App Impact:
- App currently states surgery is "considered for" specific indications
- Should be updated to reference ENRICH as first positive surgical trial
- Should note location-dependent benefit (lobar hemorrhages)

**Verification:** Confirmed through NEJM publication, Emory press release, and multiple review articles.

## 5. MISTIE III Trial (2019) and Follow-up

**Source:** Hanley DF, et al. Lancet. 2019;393(10175):1021-1032.
**Board Tag:** BOARD-LIKELY

### Key Findings:
- 506 patients with ICH >= 30 mL, randomized to minimally invasive catheter-based evacuation + alteplase vs medical management
- Primary outcome (mRS 0-3 at 365 days): 45% surgery vs 41% medical (p=0.33) -- NOT significant
- Key secondary finding: when residual clot reduced to <= 15 mL or >= 70% volume reduction, outcomes significantly better
- Established the concept of "threshold evacuation" -- the amount of clot removed matters more than just performing surgery

### Board Relevance:
- Important predecessor to ENRICH
- Teaching point: adequate clot evacuation volume matters
- Helps explain why ENRICH succeeded where MISTIE III did not (different surgical approach, better clot removal)

**Verification:** Confirmed through Lancet publication and PubMed.

## 6. MIND Trial (JAMA Neurology 2025)

**Source:** JAMA Neurology 2025. Presented at International Stroke Conference February 2025.
**URL:** https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jamaneurology/fullarticle/2838201
**Board Tag:** BOARD-EMERGING

### Key Findings:
- 239 patients with supratentorial ICH; predominantly deep hemorrhages (70.8%)
- Terminated early after ENRICH publication
- Achieved 80.7% median hematoma volume reduction
- Did NOT show improved clinical outcomes at 180 days
- Differences from ENRICH: higher proportion of deep hemorrhages (70.8% vs 30.7%), different surgical technique

### Board Relevance:
- Reinforces that surgical benefit may be location-dependent
- Lobar hemorrhages more likely to benefit from MIS than deep hemorrhages
- Too new for current exam cycles

**Verification:** Confirmed through JAMA Neurology publication and AHA blog coverage.

## 7. 2023 AHA/ASA SAH Guidelines

**Source:** Hoh BL, et al. Stroke. 2023;54:e314-e370.
**URL:** https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/STR.0000000000000436
**Board Tag:** BOARD-LIKELY

### Major Updates from 2012 Guidelines:
1. **Aneurysm treatment timing:** Within 24 hours (more specific than previous "as early as feasible")
2. **Care setting:** Dedicated neurocritical care units with high case volumes recommended
3. **Seizures:** New-onset: treat with ASM for 7 days. Prophylactic ASM NOT routine. May consider in high-risk: MCA aneurysm, intraparenchymal hemorrhage, high-grade SAH, hydrocephalus, cortical infarction.
4. **Vasospasm terminology:** "Delayed cerebral ischemia" (DCI) preferred over "vasospasm"
5. **Fluid management:** Euvolemia maintenance recommended. Prophylactic hypervolemia NOT recommended.
6. **Rehabilitation:** Multidisciplinary approach; early screening for cognitive/behavioral deficits recommended
7. **Long-term follow-up:** Physical, cognitive, behavioral, and QoL deficits are common and can persist; structured follow-up recommended

### Gaps in Current App Content:
- App correctly covers vasospasm timeline, nimodipine, triple-H abandonment
- App should note the 2023 guideline preference for "DCI" terminology
- App should add seizure management specifics (7-day treatment, high-risk criteria)
- App should add aneurysm treatment timeline (within 24 hours)

**Verification:** Confirmed through AHA Stroke publication and AHA professional summary.

## 8. ANNEXA-I Trial -- Factor Xa Reversal (NEJM 2024)

**Source:** Connolly SJ, et al. NEJM. 2024;390(19):1745-1755.
**URL:** https://www.nejm.org/doi/abs/10.1056/NEJMoa2313040
**Board Tag:** BOARD-LIKELY

### Study Design:
- 530 patients with factor Xa inhibitor-associated acute ICH
- Randomized to andexanet alfa vs usual care (primarily 4F-PCC)
- Enrollment: November 2017 to November 2023 (terminated early)

### Results:
- **Efficacy:** Hemostatic efficacy in 67.0% andexanet vs 53.1% usual care (p=0.003)
- **Anti-Xa activity:** 94.5% reduction with andexanet vs 26.9% with usual care
- **Safety concern:** Thrombotic events 10.3% andexanet vs 5.6% usual care; ischemic stroke 6.5% vs 1.5%
- Trial terminated early: "mixed results" -- better hemostasis but higher thrombotic risk

### Board Relevance:
- Establishes andexanet alfa as more effective than usual care for hemostasis
- But safety signal (thrombotic events, especially ischemic stroke) is significant
- Board question likely: "andexanet alfa reverses factor Xa inhibitors but carries thrombotic risk"
- 4F-PCC remains a reasonable alternative, especially in resource-limited settings

### App Impact:
- App currently lists andexanet alfa as reversal agent -- correct
- Should add ANNEXA-I trial results showing efficacy but thrombotic risk
- Should note 4F-PCC as alternative when andexanet unavailable

**Verification:** Confirmed through NEJM publication, ACC review, and multiple analysis articles.

## 9. ARUBA Trial Long-term Follow-up and Post-ARUBA Era

**Source:** Mohr JP, et al. Lancet Neurology. 2020;19(7):573-581.
**Board Tag:** BOARD-CORE (original findings); BOARD-LIKELY (post-ARUBA consequences)

### Long-term Follow-up (2020):
- Medical management alone remained superior to intervention after extended follow-up
- Reinforced original findings

### Unintended Consequences (2020-2025):
- Ruptured AVM incidence increased from 13.3% pre-ARUBA to 34.4% post-ARUBA
- In-hospital mortality increased from 2.0% to 7.6%
- Endovascular treatment for unruptured AVMs declined 34.7% (2016-2022)
- Real-world registry data (NVQI-QOD) shows MUCH better outcomes than ARUBA intervention arm with careful patient selection: death/stroke 8.7% vs 30.7%

### Current Clinical Implications:
- ARUBA results are valid but may not apply to all patients
- Low-grade AVMs (SM I-II) may still benefit from treatment
- Patient selection and treatment expertise matter enormously
- Blanket observation policy may be harmful for some patients

### App Impact:
- App correctly references ARUBA trial
- Should add post-ARUBA controversy and unintended consequences

**Verification:** Confirmed through Lancet Neurology, AHA journal publications, and PMC analyses.

## 10. Spot Sign and Hematoma Expansion Prediction

**Source:** Multiple studies and meta-analyses; 2022 AHA ICH guidelines
**Board Tag:** BOARD-LIKELY

### Current Evidence:
- CTA spot sign: sensitivity ~53%, specificity ~88% for predicting hematoma expansion
- Present in ~25-30% of ICH patients scanned within 6 hours
- Hematoma expansion occurs in ~25-38% of patients within 24 hours
- 2022 AHA guidelines: CTA can identify patients at risk who may benefit from aggressive management

### Emerging Advances:
- Multiphase CTA and dynamic CTA show improved sensitivity/specificity vs single-phase
- Dual-energy CT may detect active extravasation more reliably
- Spot sign score system (considering number, size, Hounsfield units) improves prediction

### Board Relevance:
- Likely to appear as a diagnostic imaging question
- Key concept: spot sign = active contrast extravasation = predicts growth
- Clinical application: identifies patients for more aggressive management

**Verification:** Confirmed through AHA Stroke publications, PLOS ONE meta-analysis, and multiple individual studies.

## 11. Anticoagulant-Associated ICH Reversal -- Current Evidence

**Source:** Multiple comparative studies 2022-2024
**Board Tag:** BOARD-CORE (reversal agents); BOARD-LIKELY (comparative data)

### Current Reversal Algorithm:
1. **Warfarin:** 4F-PCC (Kcentra) + IV Vitamin K 10mg. Target INR <1.4 within 1 hour. PCC preferred over FFP. [BOARD-CORE]
2. **Dabigatran:** Idarucizumab (Praxbind) 5g IV. Immediate, complete reversal. [BOARD-CORE]
3. **Factor Xa inhibitors (rivaroxaban, apixaban):** Andexanet alfa (Andexxa) if available; 4F-PCC if unavailable. [BOARD-CORE]
4. **UFH:** Protamine sulfate 1mg per 100 units. [BOARD-CORE]
5. **LMWH:** Protamine sulfate (only ~60% reversal). [BOARD-CORE]
6. **Antiplatelets:** Platelet transfusion NOT recommended (PATCH trial -- may worsen outcomes). [BOARD-CORE]

### Comparative Data (Andexanet vs 4F-PCC):
- Hemostatic efficacy: 64-80% andexanet vs 54-60% 4F-PCC
- 30-day mortality: may favor andexanet (30% vs 45% in some studies)
- Thrombotic events: higher with andexanet (10.3% vs 5.6% per ANNEXA-I)
- Cost: andexanet ~$22,120 vs 4F-PCC ~$5,670 per patient
- No clear winner; decision should consider availability, cost, and patient risk factors

**Verification:** Cross-referenced ANNEXA-I trial, multiple comparative studies, and 2022 AHA guidelines.

## 12. Rehabilitation After Hemorrhagic Stroke

**Source:** 2024 VA/DoD Stroke Rehabilitation Clinical Practice Guidelines; 2022 AHA ICH guidelines
**URL:** https://www.healthquality.va.gov/guidelines/Rehab/stroke/
**Board Tag:** BOARD-CORE (general rehab); BOARD-LIKELY (2024 VA/DoD updates)

### 2024 VA/DoD Guidelines Key Points:
- 47 evidence-based recommendations organized into 2 algorithms
- Most comprehensive and current US stroke rehabilitation guideline
- Prophylactic-dose heparin does not significantly increase ICH recurrence risk (low-quality evidence)
- Multidisciplinary, holistic approach to stroke rehabilitation management
- PM&R/physiatry consultation should begin during acute hospitalization

### Hemorrhagic Stroke-Specific Rehabilitation:
- Some evidence suggests ICH patients may recover to a greater degree than ischemic stroke patients with comparable initial deficits
- Early rehabilitation is beneficial but very early aggressive mobilization (within 24 hours per AVERT trial) is harmful
- IRF care recommended over SNF care when patients qualify
- DVT prophylaxis: pneumatic compression, NOT compression stockings
- Restarting anticoagulation: generally after 4-8 weeks with strong indication; higher rebleeding risk with lobar ICH/CAA

### App Impact:
- App does not have dedicated rehabilitation tab for hemorrhagic stroke
- Should consider adding rehabilitation-specific content for board preparation
- Key board concepts: AVERT trial (too-early mobilization harmful), IRF vs SNF, DVT prevention

**Verification:** Confirmed through VA/DoD CPG publication and AHA guidelines.

## 13. Updated Boston Criteria v2.0 for CAA (2022)

**Source:** Charidimou A, et al. Lancet Neurology. 2022.
**Board Tag:** BOARD-LIKELY

### What's New:
- Incorporates cortical superficial siderosis as a diagnostic marker (not just microbleeds)
- White matter hyperintensity patterns as supporting features
- Improved diagnostic sensitivity while maintaining specificity
- Expands from "hemorrhage-only" criteria to include non-hemorrhagic markers

### Board Relevance:
- Updated criteria may appear on future board questions
- Key addition: cortical superficial siderosis on MRI as supporting evidence for CAA
- Still fundamentally: lobar hemorrhages + cortical microbleeds + age >= 55 + no other cause

**Verification:** Confirmed through Lancet Neurology publication.
