## FINDING 2: 2024 AHA/ASA Primary Prevention of Stroke Guideline

**Source**: Bushnell CD, et al. "2024 Guideline for the Primary Prevention of Stroke: A Guideline From the American Heart Association/American Stroke Association." Stroke. 2024. PMID: 39429201. Published in Stroke journal, October 2024.

**Companion analysis**: Kasner SE, et al. "Future of Stroke Prevention: 7 Updates in the 2024 AHA/ASA Primary Prevention of Stroke Guideline." JACC: Advances. 2025. PMID: 40338758.

**Seven major update domains:**

1. **Dietary modification**: Mediterranean diet elevated to Class 1 (strong) recommendation. Potassium salt substitution formally discussed for stroke prevention. This is a significant upgrade from previous guideline versions.

2. **GLP-1 receptor agonists**: Class 1A recommendation for patients with T2DM and high-risk/established CVD. Based on SUSTAIN-6, REWIND, and other trials. This is entirely new to stroke prevention guidelines.

3. **Blood pressure targets**: Updated to align with Life's Essential 8 framework. Pregnancy-specific target: Class 1 recommendation for BP <160/110 in pregnant/postpartum patients with severe HTN to reduce fatal maternal ICH.

4. **Lipid-lowering medications**: Earlier incorporation of residual risk-lowering therapies.

5. **Antithrombotic agents**: Refined guidance on aspirin for primary prevention (narrower indications than previously).

6. **Colchicine therapy**: New inclusion -- anti-inflammatory approach for ASCVD patients.

7. **Sex-specific preventive risk assessment**: Screening for premature ovarian failure, early-onset menopause for stroke prevention in women. More intensive BP management in pregnancy.

**Board-readiness**: BOARD-LIKELY (too new for current exam cycles but will likely appear in 2-3 years; the Mediterranean diet recommendation and GLP-1 data are the most likely to be tested)

**Verification status**: VERIFIED -- published in AHA/ASA official guideline journal; peer-reviewed; widely covered at ISC 2025

## FINDING 3: TOAST Classification Limitations (2024)

**Source**: "TOAST stroke subtype classification in clinical practice: implications for the Get With The Guidelines-Stroke nationwide registry." Frontiers in Neurology. 2024. PMC: 10995393.

**Key findings:**
- TOAST classification accuracy is approximately 61% in clinical practice
- Most errors occur in large artery atherosclerosis, other determined causes, and undetermined categories
- Documentation and interpretation challenges are the primary reasons for errors
- TOAST remains the required field in the GWTG-Stroke registry despite limitations

**Alternative systems being explored:**
- Causative Classification of Stroke (CCS)
- A-S-C-O classification
- AI-assisted subtyping (using imaging and ECG data)

**Board-readiness**: BOARD-EMERGING (understanding TOAST itself is BOARD-CORE; its limitations are emerging knowledge)

**Verification status**: VERIFIED -- published in peer-reviewed journal with PMC indexing

## FINDING 4: Worsening Racial Disparities in Stroke

**Source 1**: "RACIAL DISPARITIES IN STROKE AFFECTING BLACK AMERICANS." PMC: 12323493. 2025.

**Source 2**: "Racial and Ethnic Disparities in Stroke Incidence and Outcomes." Current Cardiovascular Risk Reports, Springer Nature. 2025.

**Source 3**: "Stroke deaths and their racial disparities increased over last 20 years." ScienceDaily. April 2025.

**Source 4**: "Trends in Ethnic Disparities in Stroke Care and Long-Term Outcomes." JAMA Network Open. 2025. PMID: 39786777.

**Key findings:**
- Black-White stroke disparity is now WORSENING after decades of steady disparity
- Higher stroke incidence (not higher case fatality) drives the disparity
- Over 20 years, stroke deaths and racial disparities have increased
- Nearly 3 in 10 stroke deaths now occur at home, with increases among minorities and rural residents
- Hispanic disparities vary by region and country of origin/subpopulation
- Social determinants of health (education, poverty, language barriers, healthcare provider shortages) are positively associated with stroke prevalence

**Board-readiness**: BOARD-LIKELY (health equity is increasingly tested; the 2x risk for Black Americans is already BOARD-CORE)

**Verification status**: VERIFIED -- multiple independent sources confirm the worsening trend; published in JAMA Network Open, PMC, and Springer Nature

## FINDING 5: Global Burden of Stroke (GBD 2021)

**Source 1**: GBD 2021 Stroke Collaborators. "Global, regional, and national burden of stroke and its risk factors, 1990-2021." Lancet Neurology. 2024. Available via IHME.

**Source 2**: Feigin VL, et al. "World Stroke Organization: Global Stroke Fact Sheet 2025." International Journal of Stroke. 2025. PMC: 11786524. PMID: 39635884.

**Key statistics:**
- 2021: 11.9 million incident strokes, 93.8 million prevalent cases, 7.25 million deaths globally
- Deaths by subtype: ischemic 3.59M, ICH 3.31M, SAH 0.35M
- Stroke is 2nd leading cause of death worldwide, 3rd for death+disability (DALYs)
- 87.2% of deaths and 89.4% of DALYs in low/lower-middle income countries
- 1990-2021 increases: incidents +70%, deaths +44%, DALYs +32%
- Global cost: >US$890 billion/year (0.66% of global GDP), projected to double by 2050
- 84% of global stroke burden attributable to 23 modifiable risk factors
- Top 5 global risk factors: high SBP, particulate matter air pollution, smoking, high LDL cholesterol, household air pollution
- Metabolic risks account for 68.8% of stroke burden; behavioral 35.2%; environmental 36.7%

**Board-readiness**: BOARD-LIKELY (global data is increasingly tested; the key stat that stroke is #2 cause of death worldwide is likely to appear)

**Verification status**: VERIFIED -- GBD study is the gold standard for global disease burden data; published in Lancet Neurology

## FINDING 6: Polygenic Risk Scores for Stroke

**Source 1**: GIGASTROKE Consortium. "Diversity in genetic risk of recurrent stroke: a genome-wide association study meta-analysis." Frontiers in Stroke. 2024.

**Source 2**: "Integrating polygenic and clinical risks to improve stroke risk stratification in prospective Chinese cohorts." Science China Life Sciences. 2023.

**Source 3**: "Recurrent stroke prediction by applying a stroke polygenic risk score in the Japanese population." PMC: 11451717. 2025. PMID: 40135360.

**Key findings:**
- 18 genetic loci identified for stroke recurrence across African and European ancestries
- Key genes: PPARGC1B, CCDC3, OPRL1, MYH11
- Top vs. bottom 5% PRS: hazard ratio 3.01 in Chinese cohort
- MetaGRS approach: combines multiple GWAS of correlated risk factors into single score
- Japanese metaGRS predicted recurrence particularly in normotensive stroke patients
- Performance varies significantly by ancestry -- major limitation for clinical implementation
- Not yet validated for routine clinical use

**Board-readiness**: BOARD-EMERGING (2-3 years from exam relevance; understanding that genetics play a role is already core knowledge)

**Verification status**: VERIFIED -- published in peer-reviewed journals with PMC indexing

## FINDING 7: Emerging Stroke Biomarkers

**Source 1**: "Emerging biomarkers in ischemic stroke." OAE Publish. 2025. PMID: 40949500. PMC: 12425485.

**Source 2**: "Recent advances in stroke biomarkers -- implications for prognosis and treatment." Current Opinion in Neurology. 2025.

**Source 3**: "Biomarkers for Early Detection of Stroke: A Systematic Review." PMC: 11529901. 2024.

**Source 4**: "Blood Biomarkers in Ischemic Stroke Diagnostics and Treatment -- Future Perspectives." PMC: 11943631. 2025.

**Categories of biomarkers:**

Predictive (pre-stroke risk): IL-6, ADMA, endothelial microparticles, homocysteine, Lp(a), elevated RDW, MR-proANP (cardioembolic subtype)

Diagnostic (0-3h): GFAP, microRNAs (miR-124), D-dimer -- enable subtype differentiation

Subacute prognostic: CRP, sNfL, MMPs -- indicate neuroinflammation and tissue damage

Late prognostic: GDF-15, tau protein -- correlate with chronic damage and long-term outcomes

Lipid-specific: increased total lipids in very large HDL and increased HDL particle size associated with increased white matter damage and stroke risk; small HDL cholesterol associated with decreased risk

**Board-readiness**: BOARD-EMERGING (not yet clinically standard; may appear as "emerging concepts" questions)

**Verification status**: VERIFIED -- multiple independent systematic reviews and PMC-indexed publications

## FINDING 8: GLP-1 Receptor Agonists for Stroke Prevention

**Source 1**: "Glucagon-Like Peptide-1 Receptor Agonists in the Prevention of Ischemic Stroke: Therapeutic Potential and Mechanisms." PMC: 12527581. 2025.

**Source 2**: "Efficacy of glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonists for prevention of stroke among patients with and without diabetes: A meta-analysis with the SELECT and FLOW trials." ScienceDirect. 2025.

**Source 3**: Lincoff AM, et al. "Semaglutide and Cardiovascular Outcomes in Obesity without Diabetes." NEJM. 2023 (SELECT trial).

**Source 4**: EMA approval of semaglutide. NeurologyLive. 2025.

**Key evidence:**
- Meta-analysis of 11 CVOTs (82,140 participants): 16% relative reduction in stroke risk vs. placebo
- 15% reduction in stroke incidence; 13% reduction in nonfatal stroke
- Semaglutide received EU approval (2025) as first GLP-1 RA for stroke management -- based on SOUL trial (14% reduction in CV death/MI/stroke)
- SELECT trial: semaglutide reduced MACE in obese patients WITHOUT diabetes (NEJM 2023)
- REWIND post-hoc: dulaglutide reduced ischemic stroke (HR 0.75) in T2DM
- FLOW study: semaglutide improved renal function, suggesting kidney protection as mechanism for stroke prevention
- Stroke survivors on GLP-1 RAs had lower risk of subsequent events (AHA 2024 Scientific Sessions)

**Board-readiness**: BOARD-LIKELY (GLP-1 RAs are among the highest-profile developments in cardiovascular medicine; Class 1A recommendation in 2024 guidelines)

**Verification status**: VERIFIED -- multiple randomized controlled trials, meta-analyses, and regulatory approval; published in NEJM, AHA journals, and European regulatory filings

## FINDING 9: Colchicine for Stroke Prevention

**Source 1**: Tardif JC, et al. "Efficacy and Safety of Low-Dose Colchicine after Myocardial Infarction." NEJM. 2019 (COLCOT trial).

**Source 2**: Nidorf SM, et al. LoDoCo2 trial results. Published in NEJM.

**Source 3**: "Colchicine's Role in Cardiovascular Disease Management." Arteriosclerosis, Thrombosis, and Vascular Biology. AHA Journals. 2024. PMC: 11047118.

**Source 4**: "Long-term trials of colchicine for secondary prevention of vascular events: a meta-analysis." European Heart Journal. 2025.

**Source 5**: FDA approval of Lodoco (colchicine 0.5 mg) June 2023.

**Key evidence:**
- Lodoco (colchicine 0.5 mg daily) FDA-approved June 2023 for reducing MI, stroke, revascularization, and CV death in ASCVD patients
- COLCOT: 23% overall RRR; stroke specifically reduced by 74% (HR 0.26)
- LoDoCo2: 31% RRR in composite CV endpoint in stable CAD
- Meta-analysis: 46% stroke risk reduction (HR 0.54, 95% CI 0.34-0.86)
- Mechanism: anti-inflammatory action targeting residual inflammatory risk (targets NLRP3 inflammasome, reduces IL-1beta, IL-6, CRP)
- AHA/ASA 2024 guideline: adding colchicine to intensive statin therapy may be reasonable for ischemic stroke prevention in patients with recent MI

**Board-readiness**: BOARD-LIKELY (FDA-approved, included in 2024 AHA/ASA guidelines -- high likelihood of appearing on boards within 2-3 years)

**Verification status**: VERIFIED -- NEJM published RCTs, FDA approval, AHA guideline inclusion, EHJ meta-analysis

## FINDING 10: Updated Blood Pressure Targets

**Source 1**: SPRINT Research Group. "A Randomized Trial of Intensive versus Standard Blood-Pressure Control." NEJM. 2015.

**Source 2**: "SPRINT Revisited: Updated Results and Implications." Hypertension, AHA. 2022. PMC: 8824314.

**Source 3**: "Intensive Blood Pressure Treatment and Subclinical Brain Infarcts: A Secondary Analysis of SPRINT." PMC: 11060925. 2024.

**Source 4**: "Effect of Intensive Blood Pressure Control on Stroke: A Prespecified Secondary Analysis of the ESPRIT Trial." JACC. 2025.

**Key findings:**
- SPRINT: SBP <120 vs. <140 reduced CV events by 25%, death by 27% in high-risk patients without prior stroke/diabetes. Stroke reduction was 11% (not statistically significant as standalone)
- SPRINT secondary analysis (2024): intensive SBP treatment reduced risk of new cerebral ischemic events in patients with baseline subclinical brain infarcts
- ESPRIT (2025): SBP <120 halved hemorrhagic stroke risk (significant), but did not significantly reduce ischemic stroke risk
- SPS3 trial: SBP <130 (vs. 130-149) reduced stroke by 20% and ICH by two-thirds in lacunar stroke patients

**Board-readiness**: BOARD-LIKELY (SPRINT is well-established; ESPRIT findings are emerging)

**Verification status**: VERIFIED -- NEJM, JACC, AHA journals

## FINDING 11: Air Pollution as a Stroke Risk Factor

**Source 1**: GBD 2021 Stroke Collaborators via IHME. "Air pollution, high temperatures, and metabolic risk factors driving global increases in stroke." Lancet Neurology. 2024.

**Source 2**: "Ambient Air Pollution and Stroke: An Updated Review." Stroke, AHA Journals.

**Source 3**: "Hourly Air Pollution Exposure and Emergency Hospital Admissions for Stroke: A Multicenter Case-Crossover Study." Stroke, AHA Journals. 2024.

**Key findings:**
- Air pollution is now a TOP 5 global risk factor for stroke (per GBD 2021)
- Particulate matter air pollution contributes to 14% of death and disability from SAH specifically
- Short-term hourly exposure to PM2.5, PM10, NO2, and SO2 increases stroke hospital admissions
- Environmental risks (air pollution and lead exposure) account for 36.7% of global stroke burden
- Both ambient (outdoor) and household (indoor) air pollution are in the top 5

**Board-readiness**: BOARD-LIKELY (environmental risk factors are increasingly recognized; may appear as "non-traditional risk factor" question)

**Verification status**: VERIFIED -- GBD 2021 data in Lancet Neurology; AHA Stroke journal publications

## FINDING 12: Stroke in Young Adults -- Rising Incidence

**Source 1**: "Young stroke: An update on epidemiology, emerging risk factors, and future research directions." PMC: 12764685. 2025.

**Source 2**: CDC MMWR. "Prevalence of Stroke." 2024. Vol 73, No 20.

**Source 3**: "Global, regional, and national burden and trends of stroke among youths and young adults aged 15-39 years from 1990 to 2021: findings from the Global Burden of Disease study 2021." Frontiers in Neurology. 2025.

**Key data:**
- U.S.: stroke prevalence increased 14.6% among adults 18-44 and 15.7% among those 45-64 from 2011-2013 to 2020-2022 (CDC MMWR 2024)
- Stroke rates in adults <65 increased by 15% over the last decade
- Global trend: rising incidence in ages 15-39, particularly in high-income countries
- Contributing factors: rising traditional vascular risk factor prevalence in younger adults, opioid epidemic (associated with infective endocarditis and stroke), non-traditional risk/trigger factors
- In younger patients, age-specific stroke incidence has NOT declined -- contrasts with declining rates in older adults
- This is characterized as a paradigm shift in stroke epidemiology

**Board-readiness**: BOARD-LIKELY (the trend is well-documented and likely to appear as a "changing epidemiology" question)

**Verification status**: VERIFIED -- CDC MMWR, PMC-indexed reviews, GBD data
