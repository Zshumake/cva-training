## TOPIC 1: MCA SYNDROMES -- RECENT UPDATES

### What the App Already Covers Well
The app has comprehensive coverage of MCA motor/sensory deficits, dominant vs non-dominant hemisphere findings, branch syndromes, and Gerstmann syndrome. This content aligns with both Cuccurullo and DeLisa textbooks and is board-accurate.

### New/Updated Information
- **Embolic predominance in MCA branches:** Occlusion of MCA branches (as opposed to stem) is almost always embolic in origin. Superior division MCA is most commonly affected by embolism. [BOARD-CORE]
  - Source: DeLisa Ch. 18; Medscape 323120
- **Recovery patterns:** Arm recovery is typically worse than leg recovery in MCA strokes. Even among patients with severe arm weakness at onset, up to 11% may gain good hand function recovery. If some motor recovery is present by 4 weeks, as many as 70% will make a full or good recovery. [BOARD-CORE]
  - Source: DeLisa Ch. 18 (Copenhagen Stroke Study data)
- **Advances in ischemic stroke treatment (2024-2025):** Extended thrombectomy windows and neuroprotective strategies continue to be investigated but no neuroprotective agent has yet proven effective in humans despite animal model promise. [BOARD-LIKELY]
  - Source: PMC 12450147

### Gaps Identified in App Content
1. **No mention of anterior choroidal artery syndrome** in a dedicated section (currently embedded in brainstem tab) -- consider separating for clarity
2. **Recovery/prognosis data** is missing from the app -- Copenhagen Stroke Study milestones would be valuable for board prep
3. **MCA embolic vs thrombotic distinction** not addressed

## TOPIC 2: ACA AND PCA SYNDROMES -- RECENT UPDATES

### ACA Updates
- **Anterior communicating artery (ACoA) lesions** produce a distinct neuropsychological profile: severe anterograde amnesia, confabulation, dysexecutive problems, anosognosia, and personality change. This is distinct from distal ACA territory infarcts and is board-relevant. [BOARD-LIKELY]
  - Source: Frontiers in Human Neuroscience 2022 (PMC 9226309)
- **Gegenhalten (paratonic rigidity)** and grasp reflex are emphasized in Cuccurullo as key ACA findings but are not currently in the app content. [BOARD-CORE]
  - Source: Cuccurullo Ch. 1

### PCA Updates
- **PCA anatomy variation:** 70% both from basilar, 20-25% one from basilar/one from ICA, 5-10% both from carotids. This anatomical variation explains clinical variability. [BOARD-CORE]
  - Source: Cuccurullo Ch. 1
- **Proximal vs distal PCA distinction:** Proximal PCA occlusion can be devastating (thalamus, hippocampus, upper midbrain involvement) with stupor and chronic disability. Distal PCA produces mainly visual field deficits. [BOARD-CORE]
  - Source: Medscape 2128100; StatPearls NBK532296
- **Rehabilitation outcomes for PCA stroke:** Motor, visual, and cognitive impairments are common but good functional gains are achievable after comprehensive rehabilitation. PCA strokes rarely cause severe motor deficits but produce disabling visual and cognitive impairment requiring compensatory strategy training. [BOARD-LIKELY]
  - Source: PubMed 16271561; Medscape

### Gaps in App Content
1. **PCA anatomy variations** (70/20/10 split) not mentioned
2. **Proximal vs distal PCA** distinction absent
3. **ACoA-specific syndrome** not covered

## TOPIC 3: LACUNAR SYNDROMES -- RECENT UPDATES

### What the App Covers Well
All five classic lacunar syndromes are accurately described with correct localizations. The "no cortical signs" principle is well emphasized.

### New/Updated Information
- **Early neurological deterioration (END) in lacunar stroke (2024):** A systematic review identified that END occurs in a meaningful subset of lacunar stroke patients and may be related to branch atheromatous disease (BAD) rather than classical lipohyalinosis. BAD involves atherosclerotic plaques at the ostium of perforating arteries and may have different treatment implications. [BOARD-EMERGING]
  - Source: PMC 11669265 (2024)
- **Lipohyalinosis vs microatheroma distinction:** The usual etiology of small lacunes (3-7 mm) is lipohyalinosis, while larger lacunes (7-15 mm) are more often from microatheroma at penetrating artery origins. This pathological distinction may become clinically relevant for treatment selection. [BOARD-LIKELY]
  - Source: StatPearls NBK534206; Expert Review of Neurotherapeutics 2009
- **Hemichorea-hemiballismus** from lacunar infarcts of caudate, thalamus, or subthalamic nucleus is an additional lacunar presentation not among the classic five but appears on boards. [BOARD-CORE]
  - Source: Cuccurullo Ch. 1, Table 1-4

### Gaps in App Content
1. **Hemichorea-hemiballismus** lacunar syndrome not mentioned (Cuccurullo includes this as a 6th lacunar presentation)
2. **Branch atheromatous disease** concept not covered
3. **Ataxia and leg paralysis** lacunar syndrome (pons, midbrain, internal capsule) listed in Cuccurullo Table 1-4 but not in app

## TOPIC 4: WALLENBERG SYNDROME -- RECENT UPDATES

### What the App Covers Well
Excellent coverage of the clinical features, the absence of motor weakness, and the crossed pain/temperature pattern. Correctly identifies PICA and vertebral artery as causative vessels.

### New/Updated Information
- **Vertebral artery is the cause in 80% of cases** (8 in 10), with PICA occlusion being less common than traditionally taught. This is consistent with Cuccurullo Table 1-2A. [BOARD-CORE]
  - Source: Cuccurullo Ch. 1; StatPearls NBK551670
- **Hiccups** are a recognized feature (nucleus ambiguus involvement) mentioned in Cuccurullo but absent from app content. [BOARD-CORE]
  - Source: Cuccurullo Ch. 1
- **Prognosis is generally favorable:** Most patients achieve satisfactory ADL independence. Recovery is thought to occur via the unaffected side of the medulla. Dysphagia rehabilitation in chronic Wallenberg patients is an area of active clinical interest. [BOARD-CORE]
  - Source: StatPearls NBK551670; Cleveland Clinic; Medicine (LWW) 2024
- **Dysphagia as a persistent deficit:** While most symptoms improve, dysphagia may be the most functionally limiting long-term deficit. Speech-language pathology intervention is critical. [BOARD-CORE]
  - Source: Physiopedia; StatPearls

### Gaps in App Content
1. **Hiccups** not listed as a feature
2. **Vertebral artery predominance** (80% of cases) not explicitly stated
3. **Prognosis/rehabilitation outcomes** section missing
4. **Diplopia** not mentioned (occurs due to nystagmus)

## TOPIC 5: LOCKED-IN SYNDROME -- RECENT UPDATES

### What the App Covers Well
Core features are accurate: quadriplegia, anarthria, preserved consciousness, vertical eye movements/blinking, EEG distinction from coma.

### New/Updated Information
- **Rehabilitation outcome data (2023-2024):** After 3-6 months neurorehabilitation: 21% motor recovery, 42% regain swallowing, 28% regain verbal communication, 50% weaned off ventilator. [BOARD-LIKELY]
  - Source: StatPearls NBK559026
- **Mayo Clinic 2024 retrospective data:** Of acute basilar artery occlusion patients, 33.5% discharged to acute inpatient rehabilitation, 29.8% died or to hospice, 11% home independently. [BOARD-LIKELY]
  - Source: Journal of Stroke and Cerebrovascular Diseases 2024
- **Long-term survival:** If medically stabilized and survives first year, 5-year survival rate can reach 86%. Early recognition and supportive treatment with early rehabilitation has positive effect on outcomes. [BOARD-LIKELY]
  - Source: StatPearls NBK559026
- **Brain-computer interfaces (BCI):** Emerging communication technology for locked-in patients using EEG-based BCI systems. Not yet standard of care. [BOARD-EMERGING]
  - Source: Multiple sources (2023-2025)

### Gaps in App Content
1. **Rehabilitation outcome statistics** not included
2. **Survival data** missing
3. **Communication aids/BCI** not mentioned
4. **Incomplete vs classic locked-in** distinction not covered (incomplete = some residual voluntary movement)

## TOPIC 6: TOP OF BASILAR SYNDROME -- RECENT UPDATES

### What the App Covers Well
Good description of bilateral PCA findings, midbrain involvement, altered consciousness, and peduncular hallucinosis.

### New/Updated Information
- **2025 comprehensive study (Neurocritical Care):** Treatment data shows patients receiving both IVT and mechanical thrombectomy (47%) had better outcomes (median mRS 4) compared to MT only (32%, median mRS 5). Successful recanalization and GCS at admission are key predictors. [BOARD-LIKELY]
  - Source: Springer Neurocritical Care 2025
- **High morbidity:** Large proportion of TOBS patients are severely affected by reduced vigilance that persists without recanalization. [BOARD-CORE]
  - Source: PMC 10550261

### Gaps in App Content
1. **Treatment outcome data** for TOBS not included
2. **Thrombectomy evidence** in posterior circulation not mentioned

## TOPIC 7: MIDBRAIN SYNDROMES (WEBER/BENEDIKT/CLAUDE) -- RECENT UPDATES

### What the App Covers
Weber and Benedikt are in the brainstem syndromes table. Claude syndrome is not mentioned.

### New/Updated Information
- **Claude syndrome epidemiology (2024):** A cross-sectional study of 520 posterior circulation strokes found 48 (9.2%) had isolated mesencephalic infarcts. Claude syndrome was the most common pattern (43.8%), followed by Weber syndrome. This challenges older teaching that Weber is the most common midbrain syndrome. [BOARD-LIKELY]
  - Source: Neurology India 2024 (LWW)
- **Nothnagel syndrome:** Another midbrain syndrome (CN III palsy + ipsilateral cerebellar ataxia) from involvement of superior cerebellar peduncle in the tectum. Less commonly tested but occasionally appears. [BOARD-LIKELY]
  - Source: Neurology 1992; 42(9):1820

### Gaps in App Content
1. **Claude syndrome** not included (only Weber and Benedikt in the table)
2. **Nothnagel syndrome** not mentioned
3. **Relative frequency data** for midbrain syndromes not included

## TOPIC 8: THALAMIC PAIN SYNDROME (DEJERINE-ROUSSY) -- RECENT UPDATES

### What the App Covers Well
Good description of the evolution from sensory loss to central pain, allodynia, and treatment difficulty.

### New/Updated Information
- **Prevalence:** Affects up to 8% of all stroke survivors, not just those with thalamic infarcts. [BOARD-CORE]
  - Source: StatPearls NBK554490
- **2024 systematic review and meta-analysis (Journal of Pain):** Comprehensive evidence on CPSP management:
  - First-line: Amitriptyline or lamotrigine (TCAs have more robust evidence than anticonvulsants)
  - Second-line: Gabapentin/pregabalin
  - Opioids NOT supported by intervention research
  - rTMS targeting M1 has the most robust evidence among neuromodulation treatments
  - Motor cortex stimulation (MCS) with epidural electrode shows efficacy
  - Acupuncture, virtual reality, CBT have some supporting evidence
  [BOARD-LIKELY for treatment hierarchy]
  - Source: Journal of Pain 2024
- **Hyperbaric oxygen therapy (2024):** Case report showing benefit in thalamic pain syndrome. Still very preliminary. [BOARD-EMERGING]
  - Source: Frontiers in Neurology 2024

### Gaps in App Content
1. **Treatment hierarchy** could be more structured (first-line vs second-line)
2. **rTMS evidence** not mentioned
3. **Prevalence data** (8% of stroke survivors) not included
4. **Not limited to thalamic strokes** -- any central lesion can cause CPSP

## TOPIC 9: MEDIAL MEDULLARY SYNDROME -- RECENT UPDATES

### What the App Covers
Basic triad is in the brainstem table (CN XII + contralateral hemiparesis + proprioception loss).

### New/Updated Information
- **Rarity confirmed:** Ratio of medial to lateral medullary infarct approximately 1:10. [BOARD-CORE]
  - Source: Cuccurullo Ch. 1; StatPearls NBK560590
- **Extended clinical features:** Motor dysfunction in 91%, sensory dysfunction in 73%, vertigo/dizziness in 59% of cases. [BOARD-LIKELY]
  - Source: StatPearls NBK560590
- **DWI sensitivity:** MRI with DWI approaches 95-100% sensitivity and can detect lesions within 30 minutes of onset. [BOARD-CORE]
  - Source: StatPearls NBK560590
- **Bilateral medial medullary syndrome:** Rare but devastating (quadriparesis). Associated with Takayasu arteritis and other vasculitides. [BOARD-LIKELY]
  - Source: PMC 3762468

### Gaps in App Content
1. No dedicated section for medial medullary syndrome (only in summary table)
2. DWI imaging sensitivity data absent
3. Bilateral variant not mentioned

## TOPIC 10: CEREBELLAR ARTERY SYNDROMES (PICA/AICA/SCA) -- RECENT UPDATES

### What the App Covers
PICA is mentioned in context of Wallenberg. No dedicated AICA or SCA content.

### New/Updated Information (All BOARD-CORE unless noted)
- **AICA distinguishing feature:** Hearing loss and tinnitus (labyrinthine artery is a branch of AICA). This differentiates AICA infarcts from PICA and SCA. Ipsilateral facial paralysis (CN VII) is also characteristic.
  - Source: StatPearls NBK470416; ScienceDirect
- **SCA distinguishing features:** More severe ataxia and dysarthria, less vertigo/headache than PICA. Obtunded consciousness more frequent.
  - Source: AHA Stroke; Brains 2024
- **PICA features:** Headache is 20-30% more common than in AICA or SCA. Most common cerebellar stroke.
- **Acute cerebellar stroke emergency:** Large posterior fossa hemorrhage/infarct can compress fourth ventricle causing acute hydrocephalus. Urgent surgical decompression may be lifesaving. [BOARD-CORE]
  - Source: Cuccurullo Ch. 1

### Gaps in App Content
1. **AICA syndrome** completely absent
2. **SCA syndrome** completely absent
3. **Cerebellar stroke as surgical emergency** (hydrocephalus from fourth ventricle compression) not covered
4. **Labyrinthine artery** (AICA branch) and hearing loss as distinguishing feature not mentioned

## TOPIC 11: WATERSHED INFARCT PATTERNS -- RECENT UPDATES

### What the App Covers
Brief mention of watershed infarcts in context of Balint syndrome. No dedicated section.

### New/Updated Information (All BOARD-CORE unless noted)
- **10% of all brain infarcts** are watershed/border zone type
- **Two types:**
  - External (cortical): ACA-MCA and MCA-PCA junctions. Primarily embolic.
  - Internal (subcortical): Between deep perforators and cortical branches. Primarily hemodynamic.
- **"Man in a barrel" syndrome:** Bilateral ACA-MCA watershed causing bilateral arm weakness with preserved leg and face. Classic board question.
- **Rosary bead pattern:** Internal watershed infarcts appear as a chain of white matter lesions parallel to lateral ventricle on imaging.
- **Risk factors:** Postural hypotension, cardiac arrhythmias, excessive antihypertensives, cardiac surgery
- **2023 narrative review** provides updated diagnostic and treatment framework for watershed strokes

**Source:** RadioGraphics 2011; PMC 10478671 (2023); Wikipedia (Watershed stroke)

### Gaps in App Content
1. **No watershed infarct section** at all in current app content
2. **"Man in a barrel" syndrome** not mentioned (high-yield board question)
3. **Internal vs external watershed** distinction absent
4. **Hemodynamic failure mechanism** not discussed
