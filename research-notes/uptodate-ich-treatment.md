# Intracerebral Hemorrhage Treatment — UpToDate Summary

## Mortality
- 30-day mortality: 32-52%
- Half of all deaths occur within the first 2 days

## ICH Score (0-6 points)
- Components: GCS, hematoma volume, presence of IVH, infratentorial location, age
- Mortality by score:
  - Score 1: 13%
  - Score 3: 72%
  - Score 4: 97%
  - Score 5: 100%

## FUNC Score
- Predicts functional outcome (independence) rather than mortality

## Blood Pressure Management
- SBP 150-220 mmHg: target SBP <140 within 1 hour
- SBP >220 mmHg: target <220 first, then gradual reduction to 140-160

### BP Trials
- **INTERACT2**: trend toward benefit with intensive BP lowering (<140), improved functional outcomes on ordinal analysis
- **ATACH-2**: no benefit from <140 vs <180 target; intensive group had increased renal injury

## Anticoagulant Reversal
- Warfarin: 4-factor PCC + IV vitamin K
- Dabigatran: idarucizumab (Praxbind)
- Factor Xa inhibitors (rivaroxaban, apixaban, edoxaban): andexanet alfa (Andexxa)

## Hemostatic Therapies
- **Platelet transfusion**: NOT recommended — PATCH trial showed potential harm
- **rFVIIa**: reduces hematoma growth but does NOT improve clinical outcomes (FAST trial, FASTEST trial)
- **Tranexamic acid**: no functional outcome benefit (TICH-2 trial)

## Surgical Management
- **Cerebellar hemorrhage >3 cm** or with brainstem compression: surgical decompression indicated
- **ENRICH trial**: minimally invasive surgery improved outcomes for lobar ICH
- **STICH / STICH II**: open craniotomy showed no clear benefit for supratentorial ICH

## Seizure Management
- Prophylactic antiseizure medications NOT recommended
- Treat seizures when they occur

## ICP Management
- Head of bed elevation to 30 degrees
- Mild sedation
- Osmotic therapy: hypertonic saline or mannitol

## Infection Prevention
- Ceftriaxone 2g IV for intubated patients reduces VAP

## Key Board Pearls
- ICH score of 5 = 100% mortality
- ATACH-2 ended the push for aggressive BP lowering below 140 in ICH
- Never give platelet transfusions for ICH (PATCH trial)
- rFVIIa shrinks hematoma but does not save lives
- Cerebellar hemorrhage >3 cm is a surgical emergency
- Minimally invasive surgery (ENRICH) benefits lobar ICH; open craniotomy (STICH) does not
- Do NOT give prophylactic antiepileptics
