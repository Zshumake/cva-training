# Stroke Prognosis and Recovery — UpToDate Summary

## Mortality and Morbidity
- 30-day case fatality: 13.5-23% (varies by country/study)
- 6-month residual deficits:
  - Hemiparesis: 50%
  - Cognitive deficits: 46%
  - Depression: 35%
  - Unable to walk independently: 31%
  - Institutionalized: 26%
  - Hemianopia: 20%
  - Aphasia: 19%
- Return to work: ~50% at 6-12 months

## Prognostic Predictors
- Strongest predictors: stroke severity (NIHSS) and age
- NIHSS >16: high probability of death or severe disability
- NIHSS <=6: good recovery likely
- Each additional NIHSS point decreases odds of excellent outcome by 17%
- Stroke subtype: lacunar = best short-term prognosis; cardioembolic and large artery atherosclerosis = worst

## Recovery Timeline
- Greatest recovery occurs in first 3-6 months
- Copenhagen Stroke Study: 95% of patients reach best neurologic recovery level by 11 weeks
- Aphasia recovery benchmarks:
  - Mild aphasia: maximum recovery ~2 weeks
  - Moderate aphasia: ~6 weeks
  - Severe aphasia: ~10 weeks

## Early Physical Exam Predictors
- Finger extension + shoulder abduction present at day 2 = 0.98 probability of dexterity recovery at 6 months
- Sitting balance 30 seconds + leg muscle contraction within 72 hours = 98% probability of independent ambulation

## Prognostic Scales and Scores
- Orpington Prognostic Scale
- Reding three-factor approach
- ASTRAL score
- DRAGON score
- iScore
- FUNC score (for ICH functional outcome)

## Landmark Trials in Stroke Rehabilitation
- **EXCITE trial**: Constraint-induced movement therapy (CIMT) in chronic stroke improves motor function
- **VNS-REHAB trial**: Vagus nerve stimulation paired with rehab improves upper extremity motor recovery
- **AVERT trial**: Very early mobilization (<24 hours post-stroke) is HARMFUL — associated with worse outcomes

## Key Board Pearls
- NIHSS is the single most important prognostic tool for ischemic stroke outcomes
- Early mobilization (<24h) worsens outcomes — do NOT rush mobilization
- Lacunar strokes have better short-term but similar long-term prognosis to nonlacunar strokes
- Motor recovery prediction can be made reliably within 72 hours using simple bedside exam findings
