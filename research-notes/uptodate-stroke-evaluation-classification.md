# UpToDate Research Notes: Stroke Evaluation and Classification
## Source: User-extracted facts from UpToDate

### Stroke Classification
**Two Major Types:**
- Brain ischemia (80% US): thrombosis, embolism, systemic hypoperfusion
- Brain hemorrhage (20% US): ICH (10%), SAH (3%)
- Globally: ischemia 65%, ICH 29%, SAH 6%

**TOAST Classification (Adams et al. Stroke 1993)**
- Large artery atherosclerosis
- Cardioembolism
- Small vessel occlusion (lacunar)
- Other determined etiology
- Undetermined etiology

**SSS-TOAST / CCS Classification**
- Refined TOAST with evident/probable/possible subcategories
- CCS available at ccs.mgh.harvard.edu

**ISPS25 (Yaghi et al. Stroke 2025)**
- Updated ischemic stroke phenotyping system
- Minimum evaluation: brain imaging, vascular imaging, 12-lead ECG + 24h monitoring, TTE with agitated saline, labs

### Thrombotic Stroke Features
- Symptoms fluctuate, remit, or progress in stuttering fashion
- Large vessel: extracranial and intracranial arteries
- Atherosclerosis most common cause
- Small vessel: penetrating arteries, lipohyalinosis from HTN
- Symptoms develop over hours to days

### Embolic Stroke Features
- Onset sudden, deficits maximal at start
- May clear entirely (emboli migrate/lyse)
- Multiple territories = cardiac source
- High-risk sources: AF, rheumatic valve disease, prosthetic valves, ventricular thrombus, recent MI, EF <28%, dilated cardiomyopathy, endocarditis

### ICH Features
- Gradual onset over minutes to hours (NOT abrupt like embolism)
- Symptoms related to hematoma location
- Headache/vomiting if large enough for increased ICP
- Most common causes: HTN, trauma, bleeding diatheses, amyloid angiopathy, drugs

### SAH Features
- Onset abrupt, often during activity
- "Worst headache of my life" (97%)
- Sentinel headache in 30% before major SAH
- No focal signs unless meningocerebral hemorrhage
- 25% of sudden severe headache presentations = SAH

### Epidemiology (AHA 2024 Statistics)
- US: 795,000 strokes/year (610,000 first-ever, 185,000 recurrent)
- Lifetime risk ~25% for adults >=25
- Stroke Belt: southeastern US states
- Higher risk: Black Americans (2x), Hispanic Americans
- Northern Manhattan Study: Black vs White relative rate for intracranial atherosclerosis = 5.85x
- Women higher lifetime risk than men
- Second most common cause of death and disability worldwide

### Cardiac Evaluation
- Monitor at least 24h for AF
- Cryptogenic stroke: 30-day ambulatory monitoring
- CRYSTAL AF: ICM detected AF in 8.9% vs 1.4% control at 6 months
- EMBRACE: 30-day loop recorder detected AF 16.1% vs 3.2% at study end
- STROKE-AF: ICM detected AF 12.1% vs 1.8% usual care
- TTE vs TEE: TTE first for most; TEE for <45y, suspected aortic pathology, AF, prosthetic valves

### Hypercoagulable Workup
- Low yield in unselected patients
- Indicated in: young patients, no identified cause, lupus/APS features, family history thrombosis
- aPL prevalence: lupus anticoagulant 3% (8% if <=50y), aCL 17% (21% if <=50y)
- Factor V Leiden 7%, prothrombin mutation 5%
