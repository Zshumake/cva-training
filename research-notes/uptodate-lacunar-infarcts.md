# Lacunar Infarcts — UpToDate Summary

## Definition and Epidemiology
- Size: 2-15 mm noncortical infarcts
- Caused by occlusion of a single penetrating artery
- 15-26% of all ischemic strokes
- Locations: basal ganglia, thalamus, internal capsule, corona radiata, pons

## Etiology
- Lipohyalinosis (hypertension-related): most common mechanism
- Branch atheromatous disease
- Embolism: rare but can occur
- Endothelial dysfunction

## Classic Lacunar Syndromes (5)
1. **Pure motor hemiparesis** (45-57% of lacunar strokes) — most common
2. **Pure sensory stroke**
3. **Ataxic hemiparesis**
4. **Sensorimotor stroke**
5. **Dysarthria-clumsy hand syndrome**

## Key Distinguishing Feature
- NO cortical signs: no aphasia, neglect, apraxia, hemianopia

## Capsular Warning Syndrome
- Repetitive hemiplegic TIAs predicting impending capsular infarct
- High risk of progression to completed stroke

## Imaging
- CT sensitivity: only 30-44% for lacunar infarcts
- MRI DWI: much more sensitive
- DWI overestimates lacunar infarct volume by ~40%

## Prognosis
- Better short-term prognosis: 91% favorable outcome at 3 months
- Long-term prognosis: similar to nonlacunar stroke subtypes

## Landmark Trials
- **SPS3 trial**: Long-term dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT) is HARMFUL in lacunar stroke — increased bleeding and mortality vs aspirin alone
- **WAKE-UP trial**: IVT benefit is similar for lacunar vs nonlacunar stroke (MRI-guided thrombolysis)

## Associated Conditions
- **CADASIL**: most common monogenic cause of cerebral small vessel disease
  - Notch3 gene mutation
  - Migraine with aura, subcortical infarcts, cognitive decline, mood disturbances

## Key Board Pearls
- Lacunar syndromes should have NO cortical signs — if cortical signs present, reconsider diagnosis
- Pure motor hemiparesis is the most common lacunar syndrome
- CT misses the majority of lacunar infarcts — MRI DWI is essential
- Do NOT use long-term DAPT for lacunar stroke (SPS3)
- Capsular warning syndrome = urgent — high risk of completed infarct
