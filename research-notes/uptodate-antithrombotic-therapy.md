# UpToDate Research Notes: Antithrombotic Therapy for Ischemic Stroke
## Source: User-extracted facts from UpToDate

### Early Antiplatelet Therapy

**Aspirin Alone — Indications**
- Low-risk TIA (ABCD2 <4)
- Moderate-severe stroke (NIHSS >5)
- 300-325 mg day 1, then 50-100 mg daily
- IST + CAST: aspirin reduces recurrent ischemic stroke and death
- NNT = 79 for death/dependency avoidance

**DAPT (Aspirin + Clopidogrel) — Indications**
- High-risk TIA (ABCD2 >=4)
- Minor ischemic stroke (NIHSS <=5)
- Intracranial large artery atherosclerosis (70-99% stenosis)
- Duration: 21 days for TIA/minor stroke, up to 90 days for intracranial stenosis
- Aspirin 160-325mg load + clopidogrel 300-600mg load

**Key DAPT Trials**
- CHANCE (China, n=5170): ASA+clopidogrel reduced stroke 8.2% vs 11.7%, within 24h
- POINT (international, n=4881): reduced major ischemic events 5.0% vs 6.5%, within 12h
- THALES (n=11,016): ticagrelor+ASA reduced stroke/death 5.5% vs 6.6%, within 24h
- INSPIRES (China, n=6100): ASA+clopidogrel within 72h still beneficial
- Meta-analysis: DAPT reduces recurrent stroke by ~30% vs ASA alone

**DAPT Safety**
- Major bleeding increase: ~0.2-0.4% absolute
- Long-term DAPT (>90 days) NOT recommended — MATCH, CHARISMA, SPS3 all negative
- SPS3: DAPT increased mortality in lacunar stroke patients

**CYP2C19 and Clopidogrel**
- Loss-of-function alleles (CYP2C19 *2/*3) present in ~59% of Chinese patients
- CHANCE: clopidogrel benefit only in noncarriers
- CHANCE-2: ticagrelor+ASA better than clopidogrel+ASA in CYP2C19 LOF carriers
- POINT substudy: no difference by CYP2C19 status (but smaller sample, higher loading dose)

### Early Anticoagulation

**NOT Recommended for:**
- Unselected acute ischemic stroke (IST trial, Cochrane 2021)
- Stroke due to AF (2007 meta-analysis: reduced ischemic stroke but increased sICH)
- Progressing stroke (TOAST trial negative)

**Consider Early Anticoagulation For:**
- Intracardiac thrombus (LV thrombus, mechanical valve)
- Weight-based heparin nomogram without bolus

**Timing of Anticoagulation After Stroke with AF**
- TIA: start immediately
- Small-moderate infarct: 24-96h
- Large infarct: 4-7 days
- CATALYST meta-analysis (n=5400): early DOAC (<=4 days) reduced composite of ischemic stroke/sICH at 30 days (2.1% vs 3.0%)
- Aspirin bridging if anticoagulation delayed >48h

### Long-Term Antiplatelet Therapy

**First-Line Options (all acceptable)**
- Aspirin 50-100mg daily
- Clopidogrel 75mg daily (CAPRIE: 8.7% RRR vs ASA)
- Aspirin-extended release dipyridamole 25/200mg BID (ESPS-2, ESPRIT: additive benefit)
- PRoFESS: clopidogrel ≈ ASA-dipyridamole

**Cilostazol**
- Reasonable for East Asian populations (CSPS, CSPS II trials)
- 100mg BID
- Less bleeding than aspirin

**Rivaroxaban 2.5mg BID + Aspirin**
- COMPASS trial: reduced stroke + CV death in atherosclerotic patients
- ARR 1.3% overall, 2.8% in prior stroke subgroup
- Increased major bleeding but not fatal/intracranial bleeding

### Other Key Points
- Antiplatelet treatment failure: re-evaluate mechanism, consider switching agents
- Don't stop antiplatelets abruptly (platelet lifespan ~10 days, rebound risk)
- Long-term DAPT (ASA+clopidogrel) NOT recommended: MATCH, CHARISMA, SPS3 all showed harm > benefit
