# UpToDate Research Notes: Neuroimaging of Acute Stroke
## Source: User-extracted facts from UpToDate

### Key Clinical Facts

**CT vs MRI**
- CT: widespread, fast, excellent for hemorrhage detection
- CT sensitivity for ischemia in first 6h: only 64%
- MRI DWI: detects ischemia within 3-30 min of onset
- DWI sensitivity for acute ischemic stroke: 92%
- MRI with GRE/SWI equivalent to CT for acute hemorrhage detection

**ASPECTS (Alberta Stroke Program Early CT Score)**
- 10-point scale, MCA territory
- 10 = normal, 0 = diffuse ischemic change
- Subtract 1 point per region with early ischemic change
- 10 regions: C (caudate), L (lentiform), IC (internal capsule), I (insular), M1-M6
- ASPECTS >=6 associated with benefit from thrombectomy
- Lower ASPECTS = worse outcomes
- pc-ASPECTS for posterior circulation: also 10-point scale
- Automated ASPECTS software improving reliability

**CT Perfusion (CTP)**
- Core: severely reduced CBF
- Penumbra: delayed Tmax but preserved CBV
- Ischemic field - core = penumbra (salvageable tissue)
- Used for EVT patient selection in 6-24h window
- DEFUSE 3 used CTP or MRI perfusion

**MRI Specific**
- DWI: bright signal = restricted diffusion = cytotoxic edema = infarction
- ADC map: low signal confirms true restriction (vs T2 shine-through)
- ADC nadir at 2-3 days, pseudonormalization at ~10 days
- FLAIR: becomes hyperintense ~6h after stroke
- DWI-FLAIR mismatch: DWI+ / FLAIR- = onset within ~4.5h (basis for wake-up stroke thrombolysis)

**Hyperdense Artery Sign**
- Seen on noncontrast CT in 30-40% of MCA strokes
- Highly specific for MCA occlusion
- NOT time-dependent, NOT related to ischemic injury
- Sylvian dot sign = distal MCA branch occlusion

**Susceptibility Vessel Sign (MRI)**
- Analogous to hyperdense artery on CT
- T2*-weighted GRE or SWI
- Sensitivity 83% vs MRA, specificity 100%

**Collateral Assessment**
- Multiphase CTA: 3-phase acquisition showing collateral flow
- Used in ESCAPE trial for EVT patient selection
- FLAIR hyperintense vessels = leptomeningeal collateral flow
- Good collaterals = better EVT outcomes

**Mismatch Paradigms**
- Perfusion-core mismatch (CTP or MRI PWI/DWI): core small, penumbra large
- Clinical-core mismatch (DAWN trial): NIHSS severity > expected from DWI volume
- Clinical-ASPECTS mismatch: NIHSS >=10 + ASPECTS >=6
- DWI-FLAIR mismatch: stroke onset within ~4.5h

**Imaging Selection by Time Window**
- <4.5h: noncontrast CT + CTA sufficient
- 4.5-24h: multimodal CT (CT + CTA + CTP) or multimodal MRI
- Unknown onset: MRI with DWI + FLAIR preferred
