# Stroke Scales and Outcome Measures — Key Clinical Facts

Source: UpToDate clinical review (stroke scales and clinical assessment tools)

## ICF Framework for Classifying Stroke Outcomes

- Body Functions/Structures = Impairment
- Activities = Disability (formerly)
- Participation = Handicap (formerly)

## Prehospital / Diagnostic Screening Scales

- FAST: Face, Arm, Speech, Time
- BE-FAST: adds Balance and Eyes to FAST; adopted by majority of comprehensive stroke centers
- CPSS: Cincinnati Prehospital Stroke Scale
- LAPSS: Los Angeles Prehospital Stroke Screen
- ROSIER: Recognition of Stroke in the Emergency Room

## Large Vessel Occlusion (LVO) Triage Scales

- RACE: best reported AUROC at 0.75
- LAMS, C-STAT, FAST-ED also used
- No current LVO scale achieves both high sensitivity and high specificity simultaneously

## NIHSS (NIH Stroke Scale)

- 15-item scale, score range 0-42
- Validated for both clinical practice and research
- Limitations: underrepresents posterior circulation deficits; no balance assessment
- 2024 update: "Cookie Theft" picture replaced by "Precarious Painter"; new naming items added

## NIHSS Variants

- Modified NIHSS (mNIHSS): omits LOC, facial weakness, ataxia, dysarthria; similar predictive validity
- PedNIHSS: adapted version for pediatric stroke assessment

## Canadian Neurological Scale (CNS)

- Simpler and faster to administer than NIHSS
- Captures fewer deficits overall

## Barthel Index

- 10 items, score range 0-100
- Score >60: assisted independence
- Score <40: severe dependency

## Functional Independence Measure (FIM)

- 13 motor items + 5 cognitive items
- Primary use: rehabilitation progress monitoring

## Modified Rankin Scale (mRS)

- Score range 0 (no symptoms) to 6 (death)
- Most widely used global outcome measure in stroke trials
- Limitations: weighted toward ambulation and physical function; does not capture social participation well
- Ongoing debate: shift analysis (ordinal) vs. dichotomous cutoff approach in clinical trials

## Health-Related Quality of Life

- Stroke Impact Scale (SIS): stroke-specific HRQOL measure with 8 domains

## Depression Screening

- PHQ-9: validated for primary care stroke follow-up
- Other tools used: BDI (Beck Depression Inventory), CES-D, Hamilton Rating Scale

## Domain-Specific Assessment Scales

- Fugl-Meyer Assessment: motor impairment (upper and lower extremity)
- Berg Balance Scale: balance assessment
- ARAT (Action Research Arm Test): arm and hand function
- MoCA (Montreal Cognitive Assessment): cognitive screening

## Functional / Participation Measures

- Frenchay Activities Index: IADL measure designed specifically for stroke populations

## Prognostic / Risk Scores

- ABCD2: TIA risk stratification for subsequent stroke
- ICH Score: predicts 30-day mortality after intracerebral hemorrhage
