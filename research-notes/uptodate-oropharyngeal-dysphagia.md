# Oropharyngeal Dysphagia — UpToDate Summary

## Definition
- Difficulty transferring food bolus from mouth to pharynx (oropharyngeal phase)

## Screening
- 3-oz water swallow test at admission, before any oral intake
- Must screen all stroke patients before giving food, liquids, or oral medications

## Diagnostic Evaluation
- **VFSS (videofluoroscopic swallowing study)**: gold standard for detailed swallowing assessment
- **FEES (fiberoptic endoscopic evaluation of swallowing)**: portable, bedside, good for detecting structural lesions
- VFSS vs FEES: good correlation overall, but FEES may overestimate aspiration severity

## Independent Predictors of Post-Stroke Dysphagia
- Facial palsy
- Aphasia
- Age >70
- Stroke severity (NIHSS)
- Impaired pharyngeal response

## Natural History
- 90% of post-stroke dysphagia recovers spontaneously within 2 weeks

## Treatment Approaches

### Dietary Modifications
- Thickened liquids (thin liquids are highest aspiration risk)
- Bolus size reduction
- Texture modification of solids

### Compensatory Swallowing Techniques
- **Chin tuck**: protects airway during swallow
- **Head rotation to weak side**: directs bolus to stronger pharyngeal channel
- **Supraglottic swallow**: voluntary airway closure before and during swallow
- **Mendelsohn maneuver**: prolonged laryngeal elevation to keep UES open

### Interventional Options
- **Cricopharyngeal myotomy**: for primary cricopharyngeal dysfunction, ~60% response rate
- **Botulinum toxin injection**: for cricopharyngeal dysfunction in non-surgical candidates
- **Neuromuscular electrical stimulation (NMES)**: limited evidence, small benefit only

## Enteral Nutrition Decisions
- Dysphagia expected >4 weeks: PEG tube placement
- Dysphagia expected <4 weeks: nasogastric tube as bridge

## Key Board Pearls
- VFSS is the gold standard for dysphagia evaluation
- FEES is the best bedside alternative (portable, no radiation)
- 90% of post-stroke dysphagia resolves within 2 weeks
- Chin tuck and head rotation are the most commonly tested compensatory techniques
- PEG if dysphagia >4 weeks; NG tube if <4 weeks
- Cricopharyngeal myotomy has ~60% response rate
- Screen BEFORE any oral intake — dysphagia screening reduces aspiration pneumonia
