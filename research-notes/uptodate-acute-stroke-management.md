# UpToDate Research Notes: Initial Assessment and Management of Acute Stroke
## Source: User-extracted facts from UpToDate (not verbatim prose)

### Key Clinical Facts Extracted

**2026 AHA/ASA Guideline Update (Prabhakaran et al. Stroke 2026)**
- Expanded time window for IV thrombolysis with advanced imaging
- Tenecteplase OR alteplase now both recommended
- Expanded eligibility for EVT including large core infarcts and basilar artery
- Dual antiplatelet therapy endorsed
- Mobile stroke units recommended where available
- Telestroke/teleradiology for rapid imaging interpretation

**Prehospital Assessment**
- FAST, Cincinnati Prehospital Stroke Scale (CPSS), LAPSS for stroke identification
- Large vessel occlusion triage scales: RACE, LAMS, C-STAT
- EMS should prioritize transport to nearest stroke-capable hospital

**Initial Assessment Goals**
- Determine candidacy for IV thrombolysis or EVT
- Ensure medical stability (ABCs)
- Reverse contributing conditions
- Uncover pathophysiologic basis

**NIHSS**
- 11 items, score 0-42
- Cutpoints: <5 mild, 5-9 moderate, >=10 severe
- Three most predictive findings: facial paresis, arm drift/weakness, abnormal speech
- NIHSS score correlates with outcome
- Does NOT capture all posterior circulation deficits well

**Immediate Labs Required**
- Noncontrast brain CT or MRI
- Finger stick blood glucose (ONLY mandatory test before tPA)
- Oxygen saturation
- CTA head/neck with CT perfusion for thrombectomy candidates
- DWI-FLAIR mismatch for wake-up stroke timing
- ECG, CBC, troponin, PT/INR, aPTT

**Blood Pressure Management - Ischemic Stroke**
- Pre-tPA: SBP <=185, DBP <=110
- Post-tPA: maintain <180/105 for 24 hours
- No reperfusion therapy: treat only if SBP >220 or DBP >120
- When treating: lower by ~15% in first 24 hours
- First-line IV agents: labetalol, nicardipine, clevidipine
- Avoid nitroprusside (increases ICP risk)
- Avoid rapid nifedipine (precipitous drop)
- After acute phase (48-72h): resume antihypertensives if BP >140/90

**Blood Pressure - Key Trial Data**
- U-shaped relationship between BP and outcomes (International Stroke Trial)
- Lowering BP in first 24h associated with clinical deterioration in observational studies
- CATIS, SCAST, COSSACS, ENOS: early BP reduction did not improve functional outcome
- ENOS: stopping antihypertensives associated with worse outcomes including cognition

**Glucose Management**
- Hypoglycemia (<60 mg/dL): correct rapidly, can mimic stroke
- Hyperglycemia: target 140-180 mg/dL
- SHINE trial: intensive insulin (80-130) vs standard (80-179) - NO difference in outcomes, MORE hypoglycemia with intensive
- Severe hyperglycemia (>400) can be stroke mimic

**Temperature**
- Maintain normothermia for first several days
- Fever worsens outcomes
- Induced hypothermia NOT recommended outside trials
- INTREPID trial (Greer et al. JAMA 2024)

**Head Position**
- Elevate HOB 30 degrees if risk of elevated ICP, aspiration, or cardiopulmonary issues
- HeadPoST trial: lying flat vs 30 degrees - no difference in outcomes
- ZODIAC trial: 0 vs 30 degrees before thrombectomy (stopped early, 0 degrees better but limited confidence)

**Mobilization**
- AVERT trial: very early mobilization (<24h) REDUCED odds of favorable outcome
- Mobilize after 24 hours for stable patients

**Swallowing**
- NPO until swallowing assessed
- Aspirin can be given rectally if NPO

**Stroke Unit Care**
- Dedicated stroke unit associated with better outcomes (Cochrane 2020)

**Reperfusion Therapy**
- IV thrombolysis: first-line for eligible patients within time window
- EVT: for large artery occlusion, anterior circulation or basilar, up to 24 hours
- Aspirin within 24-48h but NOT in first 24h after tPA

**DVT Prophylaxis**
- All patients with restricted mobility need VTE prophylaxis

**Statins**
- Start or continue ASAP after acute ischemic stroke
- Statin discontinuation during hospitalization associated with decreased survival (Flint et al. 2012)

**SSRIs for Motor Recovery**
- FLAME trial: fluoxetine enhanced motor recovery
- BUT: FOCUS, AFFINITY, EFFECTS trials (total >5900 patients): NO benefit on functional outcome
- Fluoxetine increased risk of bone fractures, falls, seizures, hyponatremia

**Delirium**
- Post-stroke delirium rate ~25%
- Risk factors: pre-existing cognitive decline, infection, greater stroke severity

**Neuroprotection**
- No agents proven effective yet
- Nerinetide, remote ischemic conditioning, minocycline showing signals
