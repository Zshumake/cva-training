# Neuropalliative Care in Stroke — Key Clinical Facts

Source: UpToDate clinical review (neuropalliative care)

## Core Concepts

- Palliative care is NOT synonymous with hospice; it can begin at stroke presentation concurrent with acute treatment
- Stroke kills ~5 million people globally per year and is the leading cause of chronic disability
- Mortality: 28-day ~28%, 1-year ~41%, 5-year ~60%

## Unique Stroke Trajectory

- Sudden onset with no preparation time (unlike cancer or progressive neurologic disease)
- Most patients are unable to participate in their own care decisions at the time they are most needed
- Surrogate decision-makers must be engaged early and often

## Primary Palliative Care Skills for Stroke Clinicians

- Pain management
- Serious illness communication
- Care coordination across settings (ICU, acute care, rehab, home)

## Triggers for Serious Illness Conversations

- Age >=80 and hospitalized for stroke
- Requiring mechanical ventilation
- Predicted major neurologic deficits
- Requiring long-term artificial nutrition (PEG, etc.)

## Advance Care Planning

- Disability paradox: patients and surrogates tend to underestimate the ability to adjust to a new health state
- Pre-stroke advance directives often do not reflect preferences once disability is experienced

## Pain After Stroke

- Approximately 30% of stroke survivors experience significant pain
- Headache: 6-44% in ischemic stroke
- Central post-stroke pain (thalamic pain syndrome)
- Spasticity-related pain
- Hemiplegic shoulder pain (subluxation, impingement)

## Central Post-Stroke Pain — Pharmacotherapy

- First-line: gabapentin or pregabalin
- Second-line: lamotrigine
- Historical/alternative: amitriptyline (older evidence base)

## Psychiatric Sequelae

- Poststroke depression: ~31% within 5 years
- Poststroke anxiety: ~25%

## Caregiver Impact

- Caregiver burden is higher for stroke than for cancer or dementia
- Emotional, physical, and financial toll on family members

## End-of-Life Patterns

- Most deaths after stroke occur in the setting of withdrawal of life-sustaining treatment (not acute herniation)
- Time-limited trials are a strategy for reducing prognostic uncertainty while allowing recovery time

## Prognostic Communication

- Best-case / worst-case scenario framework recommended for discussing prognosis with families
- Avoids false precision while conveying range of possible outcomes

## Hospice Eligibility Criteria (General)

- Karnofsky Performance Status <=40%
- Weight loss >10% over 6 months
- Serum albumin <2.5 g/dL

## ICH-Specific Hospice Imaging Criteria

- Infratentorial hemorrhage >=20 mL
- Supratentorial hemorrhage >=50 mL
- Intraventricular hemorrhage (IVH) present
- Midline shift >=1.5 cm
