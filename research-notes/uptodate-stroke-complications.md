# Stroke Complications — UpToDate Summary

## Dysphagia
- Prevalence: 37-78% depending on assessment method
- Water swallow test: sensitivity 64-79%, specificity 61-81%
- Formal dysphagia screening reduces aspiration pneumonia (2.4% vs 5.4%)

## Infectious Complications
- Pneumonia: 3-10%
- UTI: 11-15%
- Ceftriaxone 2g IV within 12 hours of intubation reduces VAP and mortality

## Urinary Complications
- Urinary incontinence: 32-79% on admission, resolves to ~13% by 2 years
- Most common urodynamic finding: detrusor hyperreflexia
- Indwelling catheters increase UTI risk — avoid when possible

## GI Complications
- GI bleeding: 1.1-3%
- Stress ulcer prophylaxis NOT routine — reserve for ICU patients

## Falls and Fractures
- Falls in 25% of stroke survivors within 30 months
- Hip fracture risk: 2-4x general population

## Neuropsychiatric Complications
- Depression: 18-61%
  - Risk factors: stroke severity, disability, cognitive impairment
- Delirium: ~25% prevalence

## Cardiac Complications
- Myocardial infarction: 1-2% during hospitalization

## Post-Stroke Seizures
- Early seizures (<2 weeks): 8-15%
- Poststroke epilepsy at 1 year: 2.6-4%

## Early Neurologic Deterioration
- Occurs in 2-38% of patients

## Key Board Pearls
- Always screen for dysphagia before any oral intake
- Detrusor hyperreflexia is the most common urodynamic finding post-stroke
- Avoid indwelling catheters — intermittent catheterization preferred
- Stress ulcer prophylaxis is NOT indicated for routine stroke patients (only ICU)
- Ceftriaxone prophylaxis for intubated stroke patients reduces VAP
- Depression is extremely common and underdiagnosed — screen all patients
